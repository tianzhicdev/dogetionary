Afflatus

Pronunciation:/əˈfleɪtəs/
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 灵感
  • 启示
  • 鼓舞

Definitions

noun

Definition

a divine creative impulse or inspiration, particularly in artistic endeavors.

In ZH

一种神圣的创造冲动或灵感，特别是在艺术工作中。

Examples

  • The poet claimed to have received an afflatus during a walk in nature.
  • Afflatus struck her suddenly as she stared at the blank canvas.
  • Many artists believe that their best work is inspired by an afflatus.
  • He felt an afflatus while listening to the symphony, leading him to compose his own music.
  • The novel was written in a burst of afflatus, fueled by her passion for storytelling.

Cultural Notes

The concept of afflatus has roots in Latin, referring to a divine wind or breath that inspires creativity.
