Allergies

EN→ZH

What Does Allergies Mean?

  • 过敏
  • 过敏症

Allergies Pronunciation

ˈæl.ər.dʒiz

Allergies Definition

noun

Definition

A condition in which the immune system reacts abnormally to a foreign substance, such as pollen, dust, or certain foods.

In ZH

一种免疫系统对外来物质（如花粉、灰尘或某些食物）异常反应的状态。

How to Use Allergies in a Sentence

  • I told my friends I was allergic to cats, but they brought one to the party anyway. Now I need to choose between sneezing or finding a new friend!
  • After eating a peanut butter sandwich, I realized I had allergies. I guess I won’t be joining the ‘Peanut Butter Lovers Club’ anytime soon!
  • My aunt thought she was allergic to the grass. It turns out she just needed a new lawnmower!

Cultural Notes

In many cultures, allergies are often discussed in relation to foods, pets, and seasonal changes. In recent years, the prevalence of allergies has increased and is a common topic, especially in spring when pollen is high.

plural noun

Definition

Multiple instances of allergic reactions or conditions affecting different substances or environments.

In ZH

影响不同物质或环境的多次过敏反应或状态。

How to Use Allergies in a Sentence

  • My allergies are so mixed up; I’m allergic to pollen, dust, and even my neighbor's perfume!
  • They say allergies can be seasonal, but mine seem to be all year-round, especially when I visit my in-laws!
  • With all my allergies, going to a buffet is like playing Russian roulette with food!

Cultural Notes

People often joke about their allergies, especially to common allergens like nuts or gluten. Many find humor in the struggle of navigating social situations with food allergies.
