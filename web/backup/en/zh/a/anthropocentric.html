Anthropocentric

EN→ZH

What Does Anthropocentric Mean?

  • 人类中心主义
  • 人本主义

Anthropocentric Pronunciation

/ˌæn.θroʊ.pəˈsɛn.trɪk/

Anthropocentric Definition

adjective

Definition

Regarding humankind as the central or most important element of existence, especially in philosophical, ethical, or environmental contexts.

In ZH

将人类视为存在的中心或最重要元素，尤其在哲学、伦理或环境背景下。

How to Use Anthropocentric in a Sentence

  • In an anthropocentric view, nature is often seen as a resource for human benefit.
  • Critics argue that anthropocentric perspectives neglect the intrinsic value of non-human life.
  • Anthropocentric policies can lead to environmental degradation.
  • Many philosophers challenge anthropocentric ethics in favor of a more ecocentric approach.
  • The anthropocentric worldview has influenced much of Western thought throughout history.

Synonyms for Anthropocentric

  • Antagonistic
  • Antebellum
  • Antediluvian
  • Antepenultimate
  • Anthropocene
