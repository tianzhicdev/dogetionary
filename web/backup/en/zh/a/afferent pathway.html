Afferent Pathway

EN→ZH

What Does Afferent Pathway Mean?

  • 传入通路
  • 感觉通路
  • 传入神经通路

Afferent Pathway Pronunciation

ˈæf.ər.ənt ˈpæθ.weɪ

Afferent Pathway Definition

noun

Definition

Afferent pathway refers to a neural pathway that carries sensory information from the body to the central nervous system (CNS).

In ZH

传入通路指的是将身体的感觉信息从身体传递到中枢神经系统（CNS）的神经通路。

How to Use Afferent Pathway in a Sentence

  • When I accidentally touched a hot stove, my afferent pathway sent a message to my brain faster than my brain could say 'ouch!'
  • I tried to explain to my dog that his afferent pathways are why he can hear the treat bag rustling, but he just stared at me like I was speaking Martian.
  • After eating too much pizza and regretting my life choices, my body's afferent pathways were in overdrive, sending signals to my brain about my poor decisions!

Cultural Notes

In neuroscience, understanding the role of afferent pathways is crucial for studying how we perceive and respond to the environment. It's a fundamental concept in anatomy and physiology.

noun

Definition

In a broader sense, afferent pathways can be used metaphorically to describe how information is received and processed in any system, not just biological ones.

In ZH

在更广泛的意义上，传入通路可以用作隐喻，描述任何系统中信息是如何接收和处理的，而不仅仅是生物系统。

How to Use Afferent Pathway in a Sentence

  • If only my inbox had an afferent pathway to my brain, I could finally process all the unread emails without losing my mind!
  • Think of social media like an afferent pathway; it brings all sorts of information straight to your consciousness, sometimes too fast for your sanity!
  • My ability to binge-watch shows has created a strong 'afferent pathway' between my couch and my remote control - it's a relationship built on comfort and snacks!

Cultural Notes

This metaphorical use can be seen in discussions around technology and communications, where the flow of information mimics biological processes.
