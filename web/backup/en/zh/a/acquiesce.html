Acquiesce

EN→ZH

What Does Acquiesce Mean?

  • 默许
  • 顺从
  • 听任

Acquiesce Pronunciation

/ˌakwēˈes/

Acquiesce Definition

verb

Definition

To accept something reluctantly but without protest.

In ZH

不情愿地接受某事但不作反对。

How to Use Acquiesce in a Sentence

  • She acquiesced to his demands without arguing.
  • The government seemed to acquiesce to the pressures from the public.
  • He vowed never to acquiesce to unjust treatment.
  • Despite her reservations, she decided to acquiesce in the plan.
  • They expected him to acquiesce, but he stood firm against the proposal.

Synonyms for Acquiesce

  • Acquiescence
  • Acquiescent
  • Acquittal
