Adynaton

Pronunciation:/ˌæd.ɪˈnaː.tɒn/
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 无可能性
  • 不可实现的事
  • 不切实际的说法

Definitions

noun

Definition

A figure of speech that creates an exaggeration so extreme that it implies impossibility.

In ZH

一种修辞手法，通过极端的夸张表明不可能性。

Examples

  • It was an adynaton when he said he could swim across the ocean in a minute.
  • The statement 'I'll never be happy until the sun rises in the west' is an adynaton.
  • Her claim was an adynaton, as it seemed impossible for anyone to achieve.
  • Using adynaton in poetry can create a powerful impact on the reader.
  • He used adynaton to emphasize the impossibility of the situation.

Cultural Notes

Commonly used in rhetorical contexts and literature to express strong emotions or ideas.
