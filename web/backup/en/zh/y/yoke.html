Yoke

EN→ZH

What Does Yoke Mean?

  • 轭
  • 栓
  • 耦合
  • 负担

Yoke Pronunciation

/joʊk/

Yoke Definition

noun

Definition

A wooden crosspiece that is fastened over the necks of two animals (usually oxen) and attached to the plow or cart that they are to pull.

In ZH

一种木制横梁，固定在两头动物（通常是牛）的脖子上，并连接到它们要拉的犁或小车上。

How to Use Yoke in a Sentence

  • The farmer attached the yoke to the two oxen before beginning to plow the field.
  • Using a yoke allows the animals to work together more effectively.
  • In some cultures, a yoke symbolizes partnership and cooperation.
  • The ancient Egyptians used a yoke to pull their plows in the fertile Nile Valley.
  • The yoke is an essential tool in traditional farming practices.
verb

Definition

To put a yoke on (an animal) or to join together (as a pair).

In ZH

将轭放在（动物）上，或将（作为一对）连接在一起。

How to Use Yoke in a Sentence

  • We need to yoke the horses before the race starts.
  • The two ideas were yoked together in the author's argument.
  • Farmers yoke the animals to maximize their pulling power.
  • In the story, the two lovers were yoked by fate.
  • The designer yoked different styles to create a unique fashion.
