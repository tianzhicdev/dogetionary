Quid Pro Quo

EN→ZH

What Does Quid Pro Quo Mean?

  • 互惠互利
  • 对价

Quid Pro Quo Pronunciation

/kwɪd proʊ kweɪ/

Quid Pro Quo Definition

noun

Definition

A favor or advantage granted in return for something.

In ZH

相互给予的好处或优势。

How to Use Quid Pro Quo in a Sentence

  • The agreement was a quid pro quo; he would help her with the project in exchange for her assistance later.
  • In politics, a quid pro quo often raises ethical concerns.
  • She made it clear that her support was a quid pro quo for the committee's backing on her proposal.
  • The lawyer explained that a quid pro quo arrangement can sometimes lead to legal issues.
  • They entered into a quid pro quo agreement to ensure both parties benefited from the transaction.

Cultural Notes

The phrase is often used in legal and business contexts.

Synonyms for Quid Pro Quo

  • Quicksilver
  • Quiddity
  • Quidnunc
