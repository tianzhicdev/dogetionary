Devoted

Pronunciation:dɪˈvoʊtɪd
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 奉献的
  • 忠诚的
  • 专心的
  • 倾心的

Definitions

adjective

Definition

Loyal and dedicated to a cause or person; showing deep commitment or affection.

In ZH

忠诚和专心于某种事业或人；表现出深厚的承诺或感情。

Examples

  • After the unexpected resignation of a major political figure, his devoted supporters are organizing a bake sale to raise funds for his campaign, which accidentally turned into a cookie monster convention!
  • She is so devoted to her cat that she built a tiny throne for it—complete with a tiny crown—right in the middle of her living room, making it the most pampered feline in the neighborhood!
  • His devoted attention to recycling has led him to collect all types of ‘trash,’ which his friends now lovingly refer to as ‘art’—one man's trash really is another's gallery!

Cultural Notes

In many cultures, being devoted is seen as a virtue, especially in relationships and family. In Western culture, the term can also imply a sense of sacrifice, whereas in some Eastern cultures, it may reflect loyalty to family or community. Be aware that over-devotion to a cause can sometimes be viewed as fanaticism.
