Disclosing

Pronunciation:dɪsˈkloʊzɪŋ
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 披露
  • 透露
  • 揭示

Definitions

verb

Definition

To make something known or public that was previously secret or unknown.

In ZH

使之前秘密或未知的事情变得为人所知或公开。

Examples

  • The company is disclosing its financial results next week.
  • She is disclosing the details of her plan to her team.
  • Disclosing personal information online can lead to privacy issues.

Cultural Notes

In many cultures, disclosing sensitive information is a matter of trust. In a business context, disclosure is often governed by regulations to ensure transparency.

noun

Definition

The act of revealing or making known information that was previously private or hidden.

In ZH

揭示或公开之前隐私或隐藏信息的行为。

Examples

  • The disclosure of the documents raised many questions.
  • He was praised for his full disclosure of his past mistakes.
  • The policy requires complete disclosure of any conflicts of interest.

Cultural Notes

Disclosure is essential in various fields, especially in law and finance, where transparency is critical to maintain trust.
