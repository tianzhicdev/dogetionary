Deviate

EN→ZH

What Does Deviate Mean?

  • 偏离
  • 背离
  • 脱离

Deviate Pronunciation

/ˈdiːvieɪt/

Deviate Definition

verb

Definition

to depart from an established course or norm

In ZH

偏离既定的方向或标准

How to Use Deviate in a Sentence

  • You should not deviate from the plan without consulting the team.
  • She tends to deviate from the expected behavior in social situations.
  • The road deviates slightly to the left ahead.
  • In order to succeed, we must not deviate from our core values.
  • His research deviated from traditional methods, leading to groundbreaking results.
noun

Definition

a departure from the norm or standard

In ZH

与标准或常规的偏离

How to Use Deviate in a Sentence

  • The deviate in his behavior raised suspicions among his peers.
  • Statistical analysis often highlights any deviate from the expected results.
  • In psychology, a deviate may be someone who does not conform to societal norms.
  • Her deviate was noted by her teachers, who encouraged her creativity.
  • The study identified several deviates in the data that required further investigation.

Synonyms for Deviate
