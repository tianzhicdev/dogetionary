Doctrine

EN→ZH

What Does Doctrine Mean?

  • 教义
  • 原则
  • 主义

Doctrine Pronunciation

/ˈdɒktrɪn/

Doctrine Definition

noun

Definition

A belief or set of beliefs held and taught by a church, political party, or other group.

In ZH

由教会、政党或其他团体所持有和教授的信仰或信念集合。

How to Use Doctrine in a Sentence

  • The doctrine of the Trinity is central to Christianity.
  • Many political ideologies have their own guiding doctrines.
  • The school follows a progressive education doctrine.
  • His actions were inconsistent with the party's doctrine.
  • The doctrine of separation of church and state is important in many countries.

Synonyms for Doctrine

  • Docile
