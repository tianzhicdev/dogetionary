Debunked

EN→ZH

What Does Debunked Mean?

  • 揭穿
  • 驳斥
  • 揭露

Debunked Pronunciation

dih-buhngkt

Debunked Definition

verb

Definition

To expose the falseness or hollowness of (a myth, idea, or belief).

In ZH

揭露（一个神话、想法或信仰）的虚假或空洞之处。

How to Use Debunked in a Sentence

  • The scientist debunked the popular myth that vaccines cause autism.
  • After thorough research, the journalist debunked the claims about the new diet fad.
  • Many conspiracy theories have been debunked by credible sources.

Cultural Notes

The word 'debunk' is commonly used in discussions about myths, misconceptions, and new findings in various fields like science, health, and history. It carries a connotation of bringing truth to light.

adjective

Definition

Describing something that has been exposed as false or misleading.

In ZH

形容已经被揭穿为虚假或误导的事物。

How to Use Debunked in a Sentence

  • The debunked theory was no longer taken seriously by researchers.
  • His debunked claims left him with little credibility in the scientific community.
  • The documentary presented several debunked facts about climate change.

Cultural Notes

As an adjective, 'debunked' is often used in academic and media contexts to discuss theories or beliefs that have been disproven or shown to lack evidence.

Synonyms for Debunked

  • Debacle
  • Debauch
  • Debauchery
  • Debilitate
  • Debilitating
