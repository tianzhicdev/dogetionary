Disguise

Pronunciation:dɪsˈɡaɪz
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 伪装
  • 假装
  • 化妆
  • 隐匿

Definitions

verb

Definition

To change the appearance or manner of someone or something in order to conceal their identity or true nature.

In ZH

改变某人或某物的外观或方式，以隐藏其身份或真实本质。

Examples

  • The thief disguised himself as a delivery man to avoid capture.
  • She wore a mask to disguise her face at the carnival.
  • They tried to disguise their excitement about the surprise party.

Cultural Notes

Disguising can be seen in various cultural contexts, such as during celebrations like Halloween, where people dress up to hide their identities. In fiction, disguises are often used for plot twists.

noun

Definition

A means of altering one's appearance or behavior to conceal identity or true intentions.

In ZH

改变外观或行为以隐藏身份或真实意图的手段。

Examples

  • The spy used a disguise to infiltrate the enemy base.
  • In the movie, his disguise was so effective that no one recognized him.
  • The magician's disguise added to the mystery of his performance.

Cultural Notes

In many cultures, disguises are used in theatrical performances and festivals. They can also symbolize transformation or the hidden self in literature.
