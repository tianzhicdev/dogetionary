Dilatory

Pronunciation:/ˈdɪləˌtɔːri/
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 拖延的
  • 延迟的
  • 缓慢的

Definitions

adjective

Definition

tending to delay or procrastinate; slow to act

In ZH

倾向于拖延或延迟；行动缓慢

Examples

  • The dilatory tactics used by the opposing lawyer frustrated the judge.
  • His dilatory response to the urgent situation caused unnecessary complications.
  • She had a dilatory approach to completing her assignments, often turning them in late.
  • The committee's dilatory decision-making process angered many members.
  • The dilatory nature of the negotiations led to a breakdown in talks.
