Descent

EN→ZH

What Does Descent Mean?

  • 降落
  • 后裔
  • 血统

Descent Pronunciation

dɪˈsɛnt

Descent Definition

noun

Definition

The act of moving downward, such as a fall or decline.

In ZH

向下移动的行为，例如下降或衰退。

How to Use Descent in a Sentence

  • The descent of the airplane was smooth and well-controlled.
  • After a steep descent, we reached the valley below.
  • The hikers faced a difficult descent down the mountain.

Cultural Notes

In many cultures, the term 'descent' can refer to the physical act of going down, such as during a journey or sports activity.

noun

Definition

An ancestry or lineage; the origin of a person’s family or heritage.

In ZH

祖先或血统；一个人的家庭或血统的起源。

How to Use Descent in a Sentence

  • She traced her descent back to ancient royalty.
  • His family’s descent includes roots from several different countries.
  • Understanding your descent can provide insights into your cultural background.

Cultural Notes

Knowing one's descent is often important in many cultures for establishing identity and pride in heritage.

verb (less common)

Definition

To move or lead downward; to come down from a higher place.

In ZH

向下移动或引导；从高处下来。

How to Use Descent in a Sentence

  • The children descended the stairs quickly to see what was happening.
  • They descended into the cave to explore its depths.
  • As the sun set, the temperatures began to descend rapidly.

Cultural Notes

The verb form is less commonly used but can be found in literary or formal contexts.

Synonyms for Descent

  • Descant
  • Descry
  • Desideratum
  • Desperate
  • Despirate
