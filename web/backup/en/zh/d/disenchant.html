Disenchant

EN→ZH

What Does Disenchant Mean?

  • 破灭幻想
  • 使失去幻想
  • 使清醒

Disenchant Pronunciation

/dɪsɪnˈtʃænt/

Disenchant Definition

verb

Definition

to free from enchantment; to remove one's illusions or expectations about something, often revealing a harsh truth.

In ZH

使失去魔力；使从幻觉中解放出来；通常揭示出残酷的真相。

How to Use Disenchant in a Sentence

  • The harsh realities of life can disenchant even the most optimistic individuals.
  • After years of believing in the fairy tale, she was finally disenchantment by the truth of their relationship.
  • He felt disenchantment after discovering that his favorite band was not as talented as he had imagined.
  • Many people become disenchanted with politics after witnessing corruption firsthand.
  • The movie was supposed to be magical, but it left the audience feeling disenchantment instead.
