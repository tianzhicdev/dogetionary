Obstinate

EN→ZH

What Does Obstinate Mean?

  • 顽固的
  • 固执的
  • stubborn

Obstinate Pronunciation

/ˈɒbstɪnət/

Obstinate Definition

adjective

Definition

stubbornly refusing to change one's opinion or chosen course of action, despite attempts to persuade one to do so.

In ZH

顽固地拒绝改变自己的观点或选择的行动路线，即使有人试图说服他这样做。

How to Use Obstinate in a Sentence

  • He remained obstinate in his refusal to attend the meeting.
  • Despite the evidence presented, she was obstinate in her beliefs.
  • The obstinate child refused to eat anything but sweets.
  • His obstinate attitude frustrated his colleagues during the project.
  • She can be quite obstinate when it comes to making decisions.

Synonyms for Obstinate

  • Obscurantist
  • Obsequious
  • Obsequiousness
  • Obsequy
  • Obsolescence
