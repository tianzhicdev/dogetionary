Orphean

EN→ZH

What Does Orphean Mean?

  • 孤儿的
  • 失去父母的
  • 无依无靠的

Orphean Pronunciation

/ɔːrˈfiːən/

Orphean Definition

adjective

Definition

Relating to or characteristic of Orpheus, often associated with themes of loss and longing in art and literature.

In ZH

与奥尔菲斯有关的，通常与艺术和文学中的丧失和渴望主题相关。

How to Use Orphean in a Sentence

  • The orphean melodies filled the air with a sense of melancholy.
  • She wrote an orphean poem about her lost love.
  • His orphean style of music evokes deep emotions.
  • The painting had an orphean quality, depicting loneliness.
  • In literature, the orphean hero often seeks to recover what has been lost.

Cultural Notes

The term evokes the myth of Orpheus, a poet and musician in Greek mythology who attempted to retrieve his wife from the underworld.
