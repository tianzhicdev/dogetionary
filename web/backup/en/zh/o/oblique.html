Oblique

Pronunciation:/əˈbliːk/
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 斜的
  • 倾斜的
  • 隐晦的

Definitions

adjective

Definition

Not explicit or done in a direct way; deviously achieved.

In ZH

不明显或直接地完成; 迂回实现的。

Examples

  • His oblique remarks confused everyone in the meeting.
  • She gave an oblique nod, indicating agreement without saying a word.
  • The artist used oblique shapes to create a sense of depth.
  • The author often employs oblique references in his writing.
  • Oblique strategies can sometimes lead to unexpected solutions.
noun

Definition

A line that is slanted or at an angle; not perpendicular or parallel.

In ZH

一条倾斜或角度的线; 不是垂直或平行的。

Examples

  • The painter chose to use oblique lines to convey movement.
  • In geometry, an oblique triangle has no right angles.
  • The designer incorporated oblique patterns into the fabric design.
  • He drew an oblique line across the page for emphasis.
  • The structure was supported by oblique beams.
