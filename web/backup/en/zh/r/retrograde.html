Retrograde

Pronunciation:/ˈrɛtroʊɡreɪd/
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 逆行
  • 退步
  • 倒退

Definitions

adjective

Definition

Moving backward or returning to an earlier state.

In ZH

向后移动或返回到较早的状态。

Examples

  • The retrograde motion of planets is a well-known phenomenon.
  • In a retrograde manner, he chose to abandon the progress made.
  • The economy is showing retrograde trends after several years of growth.
  • The retrograde approach to technology is often criticized.
  • Her retrograde philosophy appealed to those longing for simpler times.
noun

Definition

A period during which a planet appears to move backward in its orbit from the perspective of Earth.

In ZH

一个行星在地球的视角下，似乎在轨道上向后移动的时期。

Examples

  • The retrograde of Mercury typically lasts for about three weeks.
  • Many astrologers believe that retrogrades can affect communication.
  • During a Venus retrograde, relationships might face challenges.
  • The retrograde can often lead to misunderstandings and delays.
  • People often reflect during a retrograde phase.
