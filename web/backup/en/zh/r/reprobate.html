Reprobate

EN→ZH

What Does Reprobate Mean?

  • 堕落者
  • 被弃者
  • 无良者

Reprobate Pronunciation

/ˈrɛprəbeɪt/

Reprobate Definition

noun

Definition

A depraved, unprincipled, or wicked person.

In ZH

一个堕落、无原则或邪恶的人。

How to Use Reprobate in a Sentence

  • He was considered a reprobate by his peers.
  • The novel's protagonist is a charming reprobate who lives on the fringes of society.
  • Many viewed him as a reprobate due to his reckless lifestyle.
  • Despite his reprobate behavior, he had a way of winning people over.
  • The community cast him out as a reprobate who brought shame to their town.
adjective

Definition

Morally unprincipled or corrupt; rejected by God or society.

In ZH

在道德上无原则或腐败；被上帝或社会拒绝。

How to Use Reprobate in a Sentence

  • His reprobate actions caused a scandal.
  • She was drawn to his reprobate charm despite her better judgment.
  • The reprobate behavior of the character reflects societal issues.
  • Living as a reprobate, he rejected all societal norms.
  • Some believe that a reprobate mind leads to destructive choices.
