Revolution

EN→ZH

What Does Revolution Mean?

  • 革命
  • 旋转
  • 改革

Revolution Pronunciation

/ˌrɛvəˈluːʃən/

Revolution Definition

noun

Definition

a forcible overthrow of a government or social order in favor of a new system.

In ZH

对政府或社会秩序的强制推翻，以支持新的系统。

How to Use Revolution in a Sentence

  • The revolution led to significant changes in the government.
  • Many people fought bravely during the revolution.
  • The American Revolution inspired other countries to seek independence.
  • The revolution was marked by widespread protests and activism.
  • During the French Revolution, many radical ideas began to take hold.
noun

Definition

a dramatic and wide-reaching change in the way something works or is organized or in people's ideas about it.

In ZH

对事物运作方式、组织结构或人们观念的戏剧性和广泛的改变。

How to Use Revolution in a Sentence

  • The digital revolution has transformed the way we communicate.
  • There has been a revolution in our understanding of the universe.
  • The industrial revolution brought about major technological advancements.
  • The agricultural revolution changed how food was produced and consumed.
  • The environmental revolution is crucial for sustainable living.
noun

Definition

the orbiting of a celestial body around another celestial body.

In ZH

天体围绕另一个天体的轨道运动。

How to Use Revolution in a Sentence

  • The Earth completes one revolution around the Sun every year.
  • The revolution of the planets is governed by gravitational forces.
  • The moon's revolution around the Earth takes about 27.3 days.
  • Scientists study the revolution of satellites to understand space dynamics.
  • The revolution of the Earth affects the changing seasons.

Synonyms for Revolution

  • Revelation
  • Reverberate
  • Reverberation
  • Reverence
  • Reverie
