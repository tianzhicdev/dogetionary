Reading Non Verbal Cues

Pronunciation:ˈriːdɪŋ nɒn ˈvɜːrbl kjuːz
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 解读非语言暗示
  • 读懂非语言信号

Definitions

noun

Definition

The ability to understand and interpret body language, facial expressions, and other non-verbal signals to communicate effectively.

In ZH

理解和解析身体语言、面部表情以及其他非语言信号以有效沟通的能力。

Examples

  • It's crucial for a teacher to be skilled at reading non-verbal cues from their students during a lesson.
  • During the negotiation, he relied on reading non-verbal cues to gauge the other party's interest.
  • When meeting new people, being aware of their non-verbal cues can help you connect better.

Cultural Notes

In Western cultures, reading non-verbal cues is often as important as verbal communication. Understanding gestures, posture, and facial expressions can enhance interpersonal interactions significantly. In contrast, some cultures may rely more heavily on verbal communication, leading to differences in interpretation of non-verbal cues.

verb

Definition

To interpret or make sense of non-verbal signals in communication.

In ZH

解读或理解交流中的非语言信号。

Examples

  • She has a talent for reading non-verbal cues, which makes her an excellent mediator.
  • Reading non-verbal cues during interviews can reveal how the candidate truly feels about the questions asked.
  • He struggled with reading non-verbal cues, which made conversations awkward at times.

Cultural Notes

In professional settings, reading non-verbal cues can help in assessing the mood or willingness of team members. In many cultures, being attentive to these cues is crucial for effective teamwork.
