Reductio

Pronunciation:/rɪˈdʌk.ti.oʊ/
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 简化
  • 归约

Definitions

noun

Definition

A method of argumentation that seeks to disprove a proposition by showing that its acceptance leads to an absurd or false conclusion.

In ZH

一种论证方法，通过显示接受某一命题会导致荒谬或错误的结论来驳斥该命题。

Examples

  • His use of reductio ad absurdum effectively illustrated the flaw in the opponent's argument.
  • The philosopher employed reductio to challenge the validity of the statement.
  • In mathematics, reductio is often used to prove a statement by contradiction.
  • She relied on the technique of reductio to highlight the consequences of the proposal.
  • During the debate, he presented a reductio that left the audience questioning the logic of the claims.

Cultural Notes

Reductio ad absurdum is a common technique in philosophical discourse and mathematical proofs.
