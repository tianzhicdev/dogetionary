Resonance

Pronunciation:/ˈrɛzənəns/
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 共鸣
  • 回声
  • 共振

Definitions

noun

Definition

The quality in a sound of being deep, full, and reverberating.

In ZH

声音的特质，深沉、圆润且回响。

Examples

  • The resonance of the cello filled the room.
  • He spoke with a resonance that commanded attention.
  • The resonance of her voice was captivating.
  • The cave was known for its remarkable resonance.
  • She felt a resonance with the music that moved her emotionally.
noun

Definition

The ability to evoke or suggest images, memories, and emotions.

In ZH

唤起或暗示图像、回忆和情感的能力。

Examples

  • The poem had a resonance with the audience's experiences.
  • Her story carried a strong resonance of loss.
  • There is a cultural resonance in the theme of the film.
  • His words had a deep resonance for those who had faced similar struggles.
  • The artwork holds a resonance that connects with many viewers.
noun

Definition

In physics, the reinforcement or prolongation of sound by reflection or synchronous vibration.

In ZH

在物理学中，通过反射或同步振动强化或延长声音的现象。

Examples

  • Understanding resonance is crucial in acoustics.
  • Many musical instruments rely on resonance to produce sound.
  • The resonance frequency of the system determines its behavior.
  • Engineers must account for resonance when designing structures.
  • Resonance can lead to increased sound amplification.
