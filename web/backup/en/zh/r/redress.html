Redress

Pronunciation:/rɪˈdrɛs/
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 补救
  • 纠正
  • 赔偿

Definitions

verb

Definition

to set right or remedy an undesirable situation

In ZH

纠正或补救不良情况

Examples

  • The government has promised to redress the grievances of the citizens.
  • She sought to redress the balance of power in the negotiation.
  • He filed a complaint to redress the unfair treatment he received.
  • The charity aims to redress social inequalities.
  • It's important to redress past wrongs to move forward.
noun

Definition

compensation for a wrong or loss

In ZH

对错误或损失的补偿

Examples

  • Seeking redress is a fundamental right in many legal systems.
  • The victims were given some form of redress after the disaster.
  • She was awarded redress for the damages incurred during the accident.
  • Redress can take many forms, including financial compensation.
  • The court's decision provided redress to those affected by the policy.
