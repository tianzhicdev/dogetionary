Microbial Basis

EN→ZH

What Does Microbial Basis Mean?

  • 微生物基础
  • 微生物的基础

Microbial Basis Pronunciation

maɪˈkroʊbiəl ˈbeɪsɪs

Microbial Basis Definition

noun

Definition

The fundamental role or underlying structure that microbes (such as bacteria and fungi) play in a particular context, often in relation to health, ecology, or technology.

In ZH

微生物在特定环境中的基本作用或基础结构，通常与健康、生态或技术相关。

How to Use Microbial Basis in a Sentence

  • The microbial basis of our gut health is so important that scientists now say you are what your bacteria eat. So, if they're munching on pizza, you might need to reconsider your diet!
  • Recent studies reveal a surprising microbial basis for why dogs often steal food - apparently, they can smell dinner from a mile away, thanks to tiny bacteria in their noses!
  • The microbial basis of our environment means that if we don’t take care of our planet, we might just end up summoning a pizza-loving fungus that eats all our leftovers!

Cultural Notes

In scientific discussions, the term 'microbial basis' is often used when referring to studies in microbiology and medicine. Awareness of one’s own microbiome has become a trending topic, especially in relation to diet and health. Just as every country has its own unique cuisine, our bodies house diverse microbes that influence our health and well-being.
