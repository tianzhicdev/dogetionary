Ministry

Pronunciation:ˈmɪnɪstri
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 部
  • ministry
  • 省

Definitions

noun

Definition

A government department headed by a minister.

In ZH

一个由部长领导的政府部门。

Examples

  • The Ministry of Health is responsible for public health policies.
  • The Ministry of Education announced new guidelines for schools.
  • In some countries, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs handles international relations.

Cultural Notes

In many English-speaking countries, ministries are critical parts of the government responsible for specific areas. Each ministry is led by a minister who is a member of the government.

noun

Definition

The clergy or the body of ministers of a religious faith.

In ZH

宗教信仰的神职人员或牧师团体。

Examples

  • The ministry of the church plays a vital role in the community.
  • He felt called to the ministry after volunteering at a local shelter.
  • Many people appreciate the ministry's outreach efforts to help the needy.

Cultural Notes

In religious contexts, 'ministry' can refer to the work done by those in leadership positions within a church or religious organization, often involving preaching, teaching, and community service.

noun

Definition

The act of serving or helping others, especially in a spiritual or religious context.

In ZH

为他人服务或帮助的行为，尤其是在精神或宗教背景下。

Examples

  • Her ministry focuses on supporting underprivileged children.
  • He dedicated his life to the ministry of service in his community.
  • The organization's ministry involves providing food and shelter to the homeless.

Cultural Notes

This definition highlights the concept of service, which is central to many religious beliefs, emphasizing the importance of helping others as a moral duty.
