Metonymy

EN→ZH

What Does Metonymy Mean?

  • 转喻
  • 提喻

Metonymy Pronunciation

/məˈtɪnəmi/

Metonymy Definition

noun

Definition

A figure of speech in which one word or phrase is substituted for another with which it is closely associated.

In ZH

一种修辞手法，其中一个词或短语被替代为与之密切相关的另一个词或短语。

How to Use Metonymy in a Sentence

  • The pen is mightier than the sword, where 'pen' refers to writing and 'sword' to military power.
  • Hollywood is known for its movie industry, using the name of a place to represent the people and culture thereof.
  • The White House issued a statement, using the name of the residence to represent the President and administration.
  • We must wait to hear from the Crown before making our decision, referring to the monarchy rather than the actual crown.
  • He earned his bread by working hard, where 'bread' stands for money and livelihood.
