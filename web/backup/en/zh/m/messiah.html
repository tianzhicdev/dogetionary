Messiah

EN→ZH

What Does Messiah Mean?

  • 弥赛亚
  • 救世主
  • 拯救者

Messiah Pronunciation

məˈsaɪə

Messiah Definition

noun

Definition

In a religious context, specifically in Judaism and Christianity, a messiah is a savior or liberator who is believed to be anointed by God to bring salvation or deliverance to a people.

In ZH

在宗教背景下，特别是在犹太教和基督教中，弥赛亚是被认为是由上帝受膏的救世主或解放者，旨在为人民带来救赎或解放。

How to Use Messiah in a Sentence

  • Many Christians believe that Jesus was the Messiah prophesied in the Old Testament.
  • The Jewish faith awaits the coming of the Messiah as a future event.
  • Throughout history, various figures have been regarded as messiahs in different cultures.
noun

Definition

A person who is regarded as a savior or liberator of a particular group or cause, often with a strong leadership quality and a following of devotees.

In ZH

被视为特定群体或事业的救世主或解放者的人，通常具有强大的领导才能和一群追随者。

How to Use Messiah in a Sentence

  • Some political leaders are seen as messiahs by their supporters, promising to bring about major change.
  • In modern culture, many celebrities are sometimes portrayed as messiahs for social issues.
  • The activist was hailed as a messiah for her passionate commitment to environmental justice.
noun

Definition

An individual who is believed to bring profound change or transformation in a specific context, often in a metaphorical sense.

In ZH

被认为在特定背景下带来深刻变化或转变的个人，通常是比喻意义。

How to Use Messiah in a Sentence

  • In the tech industry, some entrepreneurs are labeled as messiahs for their innovative ideas that change the market.
  • The visionary leader was seen as a messiah in the fight against poverty.
  • In literature, the hero often embodies the traits of a messiah, overcoming great odds to save others.
