Myths

Pronunciation:mɪθs
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 神话
  • 神话故事
  • 神秘的事物

Definitions

noun

Definition

A traditional story, especially one concerning the early history of a people or explaining a natural or social phenomenon, typically involving supernatural beings or events.

In ZH

一种传统故事，尤其是关于一个民族的早期历史或解释自然或社会现象的故事，通常涉及超自然的存在或事件。

Examples

  • In ancient Greece, myths explained the origins of the world and the gods.
  • Many cultures have their own myths to explain natural disasters.
  • The hero of the myth overcame great challenges to save his people.

Cultural Notes

Myths are an integral part of cultural heritage in many societies, serving to convey moral lessons and cultural values.

noun

Definition

A widely held but false belief or idea.

In ZH

一种广为传播但错误的信念或观念。

Examples

  • The myth that you should drink eight glasses of water a day is not scientifically proven.
  • There is a myth that bats are blind, which is not true.
  • One common myth is that money can buy happiness.

Cultural Notes

In modern contexts, 'myth' can also refer to misconceptions or stereotypes that persist in society.
