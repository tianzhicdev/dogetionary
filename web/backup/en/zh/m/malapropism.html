Malapropism

EN→ZH

What Does Malapropism Mean?

  • 误用词
  • 弄错词
  • 词语混用

Malapropism Pronunciation

/ˈmæl.əˌprɒp.ɪ.zəm/

Malapropism Definition

noun

Definition

The act of using a word in place of a similar-sounding one, often with unintentionally amusing effect.

In ZH

将一个词用在一个相似发音的词的位置上，通常会产生无意的幽默效果。

How to Use Malapropism in a Sentence

  • He made a malapropism when he said 'He is the pineapple of politeness' instead of 'pinnacle.'
  • Her use of 'dogberry' instead of 'dollop' was a classic malapropism.
  • The comedian's malapropism made the audience burst into laughter.
  • In his speech, he accidentally used a malapropism that changed the meaning entirely.
  • She knew the correct term but slipped into a malapropism during her presentation.

Synonyms for Malapropism

  • Maladroit
  • Malagasy
  • Malaise
  • Malediction
  • Malevolent
