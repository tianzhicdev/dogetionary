Narration

Pronunciation:næˈreɪʃən
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 叙述
  • 讲述
  • 旁白

Definitions

noun

Definition

The act of telling a story or recounting events, either orally or in writing.

In ZH

叙述或讲述故事的行为，可以是口头的或书面的。

Examples

  • The narration of the penguin's journey in the documentary was so dramatic that it made me feel like I was watching a soap opera about ice.
  • In the latest news report, the narration was so lively that I wondered if the news anchor had just come from a stand-up comedy show.
  • The book I read had such a quirky narration that I laughed out loud when the main character tried to bake a cake that turned into an explosion!

Cultural Notes

In storytelling traditions, narration is crucial. Whether in novels, films, or oral traditions, it shapes how stories are received. In modern media, engaging narration is often key to retaining viewers' attention.

noun

Definition

A spoken commentary accompanying a film, broadcast, or video, providing context and insight into the visuals.

In ZH

伴随电影、广播或视频的口头解说，提供对视觉效果的背景和见解。

Examples

  • The narration in the nature documentary was so soothing, I almost forgot about the fact that my cat was plotting to take over my breakfast.
  • During the live broadcast, the narration was so full of puns that it felt like a comedy club had taken over the news segment.
  • As the video showed a cat attempting to fit into a tiny box, the narration humorously suggested that the cat was channeling its inner magician.

Cultural Notes

Narration can enhance the viewer's understanding and emotional connection to the story. Great narrators can bring a script to life and add layers of meaning.
