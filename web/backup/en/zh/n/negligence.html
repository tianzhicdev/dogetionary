Negligence

Pronunciation:ˈnɛɡlɪdʒəns
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 疏忽
  • 过失

Definitions

noun

Definition

Failure to take proper care in doing something, leading to damage or injury.

In ZH

未能妥善照顾某件事情，导致损害或伤害的行为。

Examples

  • The doctor's negligence resulted in a serious mistake during surgery.
  • She was accused of negligence for not securing the ladder before climbing it.
  • The company faced legal action due to negligence in maintaining safety standards.

Cultural Notes

In legal contexts, negligence is a significant concept that can lead to lawsuits, especially in medical and workplace settings. Understanding the implications of negligence is crucial for individuals and professionals.

noun

Definition

A lack of attention or concern for something that one has a duty to care for.

In ZH

对自己有责任照顾的事情缺乏关注或关心。

Examples

  • His negligence in paying bills on time caused his credit score to drop.
  • Negligence in overseeing safety protocols can lead to workplace accidents.
  • The negligence of the management led to a decrease in employee morale.

Cultural Notes

Negligence can be seen as a moral failing in many cultures, where accountability and responsibility are highly valued. It is often discussed in terms of personal and professional ethics.
