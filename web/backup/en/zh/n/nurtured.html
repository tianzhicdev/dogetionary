Nurtured

EN→ZH

What Does Nurtured Mean?

  • 培养
  • 滋养
  • 抚育

Nurtured Pronunciation

ˈnɜːrtʃərd

Nurtured Definition

verb

Definition

to care for and encourage the growth or development of someone or something

In ZH

照顾并促进某人或某事的成长或发展

How to Use Nurtured in a Sentence

  • The teacher nurtured her students' love for reading.
  • He was nurtured by his grandparents who taught him valuable life lessons.
  • The company has nurtured many innovations over the years.

Cultural Notes

In many cultures, the idea of nurturing is associated with parental care and education. In Western contexts, nurturing often involves fostering independence as well.

adjective

Definition

having been raised or developed with care

In ZH

经过精心照料和发展

How to Use Nurtured in a Sentence

  • She is a nurtured individual with a strong sense of empathy.
  • The nurtured plants in the garden flourished beautifully.
  • Nurtured children often display better emotional intelligence.

Cultural Notes

The term 'nurtured' is often used to describe children or plants that have received special attention and care, indicating that their current state is a result of positive inputs.

Synonyms for Nurtured

  • Nursing
