Neophilia

EN→ZH

What Does Neophilia Mean?

  • 新事物喜好
  • 追求新奇
  • 新奇主义

Neophilia Pronunciation

/ˌniːəˈfɪliə/

Neophilia Definition

noun

Definition

An excessive enthusiasm for anything new, especially in terms of ideas, fashions, or trends.

In ZH

对任何新事物的极度热情，尤其是思想、时尚或潮流方面。

How to Use Neophilia in a Sentence

  • Her neophilia drives her to always seek out the latest technology.
  • In a world where neophilia is celebrated, old traditions often fade away.
  • Neophilia can lead to a constant cycle of consumption and waste.
  • He embraced his neophilia, eagerly attending every new exhibition opening.
  • The company's neophilia has brought them success by keeping their products fresh and exciting.

Cultural Notes

Neophilia is often contrasted with neophobia, which is the fear of new things. In modern society, especially among youth culture, neophilia is viewed positively, reflecting a desire for innovation and exploration.

Synonyms for Neophilia

  • Neologism
  • Neologistic
  • Neologize
  • Neophyte
  • Neoplasia
