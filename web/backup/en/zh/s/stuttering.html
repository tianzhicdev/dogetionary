Stuttering

EN→ZH

What Does Stuttering Mean?

  • 口吃
  • 结巴

Stuttering Pronunciation

ˈstʌtərɪŋ

Stuttering Definition

noun

Definition

A speech disorder characterized by involuntary pauses, repetitions, or prolongation of sounds, syllables, or words.

In ZH

一种言语障碍，特点是无意中的停顿、重复或音节、单词的延长。

How to Use Stuttering in a Sentence

  • When I tried to tell a joke about a snail, I ended up stuttering so much that I sounded like I was reciting a tongue twister!
  • During the last debate, one candidate was stuttering so badly that I thought they were trying to imitate a modem connecting to the internet!
  • My friend stutters when she's really nervous. The last time she spoke to her crush, it was like watching a cat trying to sneeze!

Cultural Notes

Stuttering is often misunderstood, and people may think it's due to nervousness or lack of intelligence, but it is a legitimate speech disorder. In some cultures, it may carry a stigma, so positive representation and understanding are essential.

verb

Definition

To speak with involuntary breaks or pauses, or to repeat sounds or words.

In ZH

以无意的停顿或重复声音或单词的方式说话。

How to Use Stuttering in a Sentence

  • I tried to explain my wild weekend, but I kept stuttering, and everyone thought I was just making up a crazy story!
  • He started stuttering when asked about his 'super secret' pizza recipe, and honestly, it sounded like a recipe for disaster!
  • Stuttering while trying to impress someone is like trying to sell ice to an Eskimo - it just doesn't work well!

Cultural Notes

It's important to approach the topic with sensitivity. Many famous people have stuttered, and it’s often portrayed in media either humorously or seriously. Awareness and education about stuttering can help reduce stigma.

Synonyms for Stuttering

  • Stultify
  • Stupefy
