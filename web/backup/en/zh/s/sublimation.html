Sublimation

EN→ZH

What Does Sublimation Mean?

  • 升华
  • 昇華
  • 提炼

Sublimation Pronunciation

/ˌsʌblɪˈmeɪʃən/

Sublimation Definition

noun

Definition

The process of converting a solid directly into a gas without passing through the liquid phase.

In ZH

将固体直接转化为气体而不经过液相的过程。

How to Use Sublimation in a Sentence

  • Sublimation occurs with substances like dry ice.
  • In chemistry, sublimation is a key process for some materials.
  • The artist described her work as a sublimation of her emotions.
  • Sublimation can be seen in the transformation of snow into vapor.
  • Certain chemicals can undergo sublimation under specific conditions.
noun

Definition

A psychological defense mechanism in which socially unacceptable impulses or idealizations are transformed into socially acceptable actions or behavior.

In ZH

一种心理防御机制，将社会上不可接受的冲动或理想化转化为社会可接受的行为或行动。

How to Use Sublimation in a Sentence

  • He used sublimation to channel his anger into painting.
  • In psychology, sublimation is considered a healthy defense mechanism.
  • Sublimation allows individuals to express their repressed feelings creatively.
  • Freud discussed sublimation as a way to cope with inner conflicts.
  • Sports can serve as a sublimation for aggressive instincts.
