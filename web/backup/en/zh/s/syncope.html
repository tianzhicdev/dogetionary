Syncope

Pronunciation:ˈsɪŋ.kə.pi
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 晕厥
  • 缺声现象
  • 音节省略

Definitions

noun

Definition

A temporary loss of consciousness caused by a fall in blood pressure.

In ZH

因血压下降而导致的短暂失去意识。

Examples

  • She experienced a syncope during the long meeting.
  • People with syncope may often feel dizzy before fainting.
  • It is important to sit down if you feel a syncope coming on.

Cultural Notes

Syncope can happen due to a variety of reasons including dehydration, stress, or standing up too quickly. It's common in older adults but can affect anyone.

noun

Definition

The omission of sounds or syllables in a word, often for ease of pronunciation.

In ZH

在一个单词中省略声音或音节，通常是为了方便发音。

Examples

  • In many dialects, syncope occurs with the word 'chocolate', often pronounced as 'choclate'.
  • Syncope can lead to changes in how words are perceived and understood.
  • Students often find syncope interesting as it shows how language evolves over time.

Cultural Notes

Syncope in linguistics is a common phenomenon that illustrates how language can change based on convenience and ease of communication. It highlights the fluid nature of spoken language compared to written language.
