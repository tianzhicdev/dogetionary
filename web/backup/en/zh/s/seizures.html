Seizures

EN→ZH

What Does Seizures Mean?

  • 癫痫发作
  • 夺取
  • seizure (拿走)

Seizures Pronunciation

ˈsiːʒərz

Seizures Definition

noun

Definition

A sudden attack of illness, especially a stroke or an epileptic fit.

In ZH

突然发作的疾病，尤其是中风或癫痫发作。

How to Use Seizures in a Sentence

  • When I heard they found a new chocolate that prevents seizures, I thought, 'Finally, a chocolate that saves lives, not just waistlines!'
  • I told my friend I had a seizure while binge-watching TV shows. He asked if I was okay or just shocked at how bad the show was!
  • After the doctor learned about my history of seizures, he said Netflix is not to blame but that I might need to watch less suspense!

Cultural Notes

In many cultures, seizures can be misunderstood and sometimes stigmatized. In Western contexts, they are often recognized as medical conditions that can be treated.

verb

Definition

To take possession of something by force.

In ZH

通过力量夺取某物。

How to Use Seizures in a Sentence

  • The cat seized the opportunity to jump on my laptop and delete my work like it was a top-secret mission!
  • When the squirrel seized my sandwich during a picnic, I realized nature had better table manners than I thought.
  • My dog seized the moment to steal my shoe, but I eventually made him drop it using the magic word 'treat.'

Cultural Notes

The verb 'to seize' can be used in various contexts, often implying a sudden or opportunistic action. In legal terms, it can refer to law enforcement taking control of property or assets.
