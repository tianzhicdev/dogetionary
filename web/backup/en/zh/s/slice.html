Slice

EN→ZH

What Does Slice Mean?

  • 片
  • 切片
  • 薄片

Slice Pronunciation

slaɪs

Slice Definition

noun

Definition

A thin, flat piece cut from a larger object, typically food.

In ZH

从一个较大物体（通常是食物）切下的薄平片。

How to Use Slice in a Sentence

  • I would like a slice of pizza, please.
  • He took a slice of bread to make a sandwich.
  • She served a slice of cake to her guests.

Cultural Notes

In many cultures, sharing food in slices (like cake or pizza) is common during gatherings or celebrations.

verb

Definition

To cut something into slices.

In ZH

把某物切成片。

How to Use Slice in a Sentence

  • Please slice the tomatoes for the salad.
  • She carefully sliced the cheese for the platter.
  • He likes to slice the fruit before serving it.

Cultural Notes

Slicing is a common preparation method in cooking, especially in Western cuisine where presentation is key.

verb

Definition

To make a quick, swift cut, often with a sharp object.

In ZH

快速、迅速地切割，通常使用锋利的物体。

How to Use Slice in a Sentence

  • He sliced through the paper with a sharp knife.
  • She sliced the air with her hands for emphasis.
  • The athlete sliced the ball perfectly for a goal.

Cultural Notes

The term 'slice' can also be used metaphorically, such as 'slicing through the competition' in sports or business contexts.

Synonyms for Slice
