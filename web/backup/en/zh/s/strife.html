Strife

EN→ZH

What Does Strife Mean?

  • 冲突
  • 争斗
  • 斗争

Strife Pronunciation

straɪf

Strife Definition

noun

Definition

A conflict, struggle, or discord between individuals or groups.

In ZH

个人或团体之间的冲突、斗争或不和。

How to Use Strife in a Sentence

  • There was a lot of strife between the two factions.
  • The strife lasted for years and led to many casualties.
  • Strife among the team members made it difficult to complete the project.

Cultural Notes

In English-speaking cultures, 'strife' is often used to describe serious disagreements or conflicts, especially in political, social, or personal contexts. It can imply a prolonged and bitter conflict.

verb

Definition

To contend or struggle in opposition; to fight or compete.

In ZH

与他人对抗或斗争；战斗或竞争。

How to Use Strife in a Sentence

  • They strife over the leadership position within the organization.
  • The community continues to strife against the injustice they face.
  • She always strives to achieve her goals despite the strife around her.

Cultural Notes

While less common as a verb, using 'strife' in this way can emphasize the effort and struggle involved in competition or conflict, often in a more elevated or literary context.

Synonyms for Strife

  • Stratagem
  • Strategy
  • Stratification
  • Streak
  • Strep Throat
