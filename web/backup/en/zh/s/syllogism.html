Syllogism

Pronunciation:/ˈsɪləˌdʒɪzəm/
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 三段论
  • 推理法
  • 演绎法

Definitions

noun

Definition

A form of reasoning in which a conclusion is drawn from two premises.

In ZH

一种推理形式，其中结论是从两个前提中得出的。

Examples

  • All humans are mortal; Socrates is a human; therefore, Socrates is mortal.
  • If all birds lay eggs, and a penguin is a bird, then a penguin lays eggs.
  • No mammals lay eggs; a dog is a mammal; therefore, a dog does not lay eggs.
  • All squares are rectangles; all rectangles have four sides; therefore, all squares have four sides.
  • If it rains, the ground will be wet; it is raining; therefore, the ground is wet.
