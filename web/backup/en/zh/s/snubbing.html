Snubbing

EN→ZH

What Does Snubbing Mean?

  • 冷落
  • 拒绝
  • 不理

Snubbing Pronunciation

/ˈsnʌbɪŋ/

Snubbing Definition

noun

Definition

The act of ignoring or showing disdain for someone, especially by not acknowledging them or by treating them in an unfriendly way.

In ZH

冷落或无视某人的行为，尤其是通过不承认他们或不友好地对待他们。

How to Use Snubbing in a Sentence

  • She felt hurt by the snubbing from her colleagues at the party.
  • His snubbing of her invitation was quite obvious.
  • The snubbing of the new employee created a tense atmosphere in the office.
  • Snubbing someone at a social event can lead to misunderstandings.
  • The actress was criticized for her snubbing of fans during the premiere.
verb

Definition

To deliberately ignore or show disdain for someone; to treat someone as unimportant.

In ZH

故意无视或轻蔑某人；将某人视为不重要。

How to Use Snubbing in a Sentence

  • He was snubbing his old friends after becoming famous.
  • She is always snubbing people who don't meet her standards.
  • Snubbing is often considered rude and hurtful in social situations.
  • He was snubbed by the group, which made him feel excluded.
  • The decision to snub the invitation was controversial.
