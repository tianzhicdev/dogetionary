Saccharine

EN→ZH

What Does Saccharine Mean?

  • 糖精
  • 甜味剂
  • 过于甜蜜的事物

Saccharine Pronunciation

/ˈsæk.ə.rɪn/

Saccharine Definition

adjective

Definition

Excessively sweet or sentimental; overly sugary in taste or in tone.

In ZH

过于甜美或感伤；在味道或语气上过于甜腻。

How to Use Saccharine in a Sentence

  • The saccharine movie left many feeling uneasy about its unrealistic portrayal of love.
  • Her saccharine smile disguised her true feelings.
  • He has a saccharine personality, which can be off-putting to some people.
  • The saccharine syrup was too sweet for my taste.
  • While some enjoy saccharine desserts, others prefer a more balanced flavor.
noun

Definition

A sweetener that is much sweeter than sugar but contains few or no calories; often used as a sugar substitute.

In ZH

一种比糖甜得多但含有少量或不含卡路里的甜味剂；通常用作糖的替代品。

How to Use Saccharine in a Sentence

  • Saccharine is often found in diet sodas as a sugar substitute.
  • Many products now use saccharine to cater to health-conscious consumers.
  • The use of saccharine as a food additive has been controversial over the years.
  • I prefer natural sweeteners to saccharine because of its chemical taste.
  • He was shocked to learn that saccharine could be bad for his health.

Synonyms for Saccharine

  • Sacrilege
  • Sacrilegious
  • Sacrosanct
