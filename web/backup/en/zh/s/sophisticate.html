Sophisticate

EN→ZH

What Does Sophisticate Mean?

  • 复杂化
  • 精致的人
  • 老练的人

Sophisticate Pronunciation

/səˈfɪstəˌkeɪt/

Sophisticate Definition

verb

Definition

To make (something) more complex or intricate.

In ZH

使（某物）变得更复杂或精致。

How to Use Sophisticate in a Sentence

  • She tried to sophisticate her argument with elaborate examples.
  • The design was sophisticated enough to impress even the critics.
  • It's important to sophisticate your methods as you gain experience.
  • He wants to sophisticate the project so it aligns with modern standards.
  • They need to sophisticate their approach for better results.
noun

Definition

A person who is educated, cultured, and knowledgeable about the world and often regarded as worldly or refined.

In ZH

一个受过教育、文化修养，且对世界有知识的人，通常被视为世故或优雅的人。

How to Use Sophisticate in a Sentence

  • As a sophisticate, she attends many high-profile events.
  • He is considered a sophisticate in the art world.
  • Sophisticates often appreciate finer details in life.
  • The party was full of sophisticates discussing global issues.
  • She felt out of place among the sophisticates at the gallery opening.

Cultural Notes

In Western cultures, the term often carries a connotation of someone who is worldly and refined, often involved in high society activities.

Synonyms for Sophisticate

  • Sophistic
  • Sophisticated
  • Sophistry
  • Sophomoric
  • Soporific
