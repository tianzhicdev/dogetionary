Xylograph

Pronunciation:/ˈzaɪlɒɡræf/
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 木刻版
  • 木刻画
  • 木版画

Definitions

noun

Definition

A print made from a block of wood that has been carved to create an image, often used for illustrations in books and art.

In ZH

一种从木块上雕刻出的图像中印刷而成的图，常用于书籍和艺术插图。

Examples

  • The xylograph was used to illustrate the ancient manuscripts.
  • He admired the intricate details of the xylograph hanging on the wall.
  • Many books from the 18th century feature beautiful xylographs.
  • The artist specialized in creating xylographs that showcased nature.
  • A xylograph can often capture textures in a unique way.

Cultural Notes

Xylography has a significant historical role in the development of printing techniques and was widely used before the invention of modern printing presses.
