Worn Out

Pronunciation:wɔrn aʊt
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 疲惫不堪
  • 用坏的
  • 耗损的

Definitions

adjective

Definition

Extremely tired or exhausted; having little energy left after a prolonged period of activity or stress.

In ZH

非常疲惫或精疲力竭；经过长时间的活动或压力后几乎没有剩余的能量。

Examples

  • After the long hike, I felt completely worn out.
  • She was worn out from studying all night for her exams.
  • The team was worn out after playing three matches in a row.

Cultural Notes

The expression is often used to describe both physical and mental exhaustion. In many cultures, acknowledging fatigue is a part of expressing one's dedication to work or activities.

adjective

Definition

Damaged or no longer usable due to extensive use; often refers to objects, clothes, or equipment.

In ZH

由于大量使用而损坏或不再可用；通常指物品、衣物或设备。

Examples

  • My old shoes are completely worn out; I need to buy a new pair.
  • The car’s tires were worn out and needed to be replaced.
  • She donated her worn out clothes to charity.

Cultural Notes

This usage can also convey a sense of nostalgia or sentimentality, as worn out items can hold significant emotional value for individuals.
