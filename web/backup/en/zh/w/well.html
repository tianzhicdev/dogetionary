Well

Pronunciation:/wɛl/
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 好
  • 井
  • 水井

Definitions

adverb

Definition

In a good or satisfactory manner.

In ZH

以好的或令人满意的方式。

Examples

  • She speaks English very well.
  • He did well on his exams.
  • They work well together.

Cultural Notes

Used to describe the quality of actions or conditions. Commonly used in everyday conversation to indicate competence or satisfaction.

adjective

Definition

In good health; free from disease or injury.

In ZH

身体健康；没有疾病或伤害。

Examples

  • I hope you're feeling well today.
  • She is not well and needs to rest.
  • It’s important to feel well before attending the event.

Cultural Notes

Often used in greetings and concerns about someone's health. Phrasing can vary slightly in different contexts.

noun

Definition

A deep hole in the ground from which water or oil can be extracted.

In ZH

可以抽取水或油的深洞。

Examples

  • They dug a well to supply water for their farm.
  • The village has a new well for clean drinking water.
  • Many old wells are now covered and forgotten.

Cultural Notes

Wells are important in many communities, especially in rural areas. They represent a vital resource for agriculture and daily needs.

interjection

Definition

Used to express surprise, hesitation, or to introduce a new idea.

In ZH

用来表达惊讶、犹豫或引入新观点。

Examples

  • Well, I didn’t expect that to happen!
  • Well, let's see what happens next.
  • Well, if you insist, I'll join you.

Cultural Notes

Commonly used to transition between thoughts or to prepare listeners for what is coming next. Very informal and widely accepted in spoken English.
