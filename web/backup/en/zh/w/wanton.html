Wanton

Pronunciation:/ˈwɒn.tən/
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 放纵的
  • 无节制的
  • 恶意的
  • 轻浮的

Definitions

adjective

Definition

Deliberate and unprovoked; showing no care for the rights or feelings of others.

In ZH

故意且无理由的；对他人的权利或感受毫不在意。

Examples

  • His wanton destruction of property led to a hefty fine.
  • The wanton disregard for safety regulations endangered everyone in the building.
  • She was criticized for her wanton lifestyle and lack of responsibility.
  • Wanton acts of violence can never be justified.
  • The film depicts wanton cruelty in a dystopian society.
noun

Definition

A lewd or lascivious person, especially a woman.

In ZH

一个放荡或好色的人，尤其是女人。

Examples

  • She was labeled a wanton by society for her promiscuity.
  • In literature, the wanton often serves as a symbol of moral decay.
  • The wanton danced provocatively at the gala, drawing attention from the crowd.
  • Critics described the character as a wanton, lacking virtue and honor.
  • Historically, wantons were often vilified in political propaganda.
