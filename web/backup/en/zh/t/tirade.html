Tirade

Pronunciation:/taɪˈreɪd/
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 长篇大论
  • 长时间的斥责
  • 激烈的演说

Definitions

noun

Definition

A long, angry speech of criticism or accusation.

In ZH

一段长而愤怒的批评或指责的演说。

Examples

  • She launched into a tirade against the government's policies.
  • His tirade lasted for over an hour and left everyone speechless.
  • After the incident, he gave a tirade that shocked his friends.
  • The teacher's tirade about homework had the students groaning.
  • Her tirade about customer service issues was hard to ignore.

Cultural Notes

In many cultures, a tirade can be seen as a passionate expression of frustration, often in a situation where the speaker feels powerless.
