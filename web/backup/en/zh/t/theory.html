Theory

Pronunciation:/ˈθɪəri/
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 理论
  • 假说
  • 思潮

Definitions

noun

Definition

A set of principles on which the practice of an activity is based or an idea to explain something, especially one that has not yet been proven.

In ZH

一组原则，基于这些原则某种活动的实践或解释某事的想法，特别是那些尚未得到证明的想法。

Examples

  • I have a theory that my cat is secretly plotting to take over the world—she's just too lazy to start!
  • Some scientists believe that if you eat cake while wearing a party hat, you’ll always have a good time, but that remains a cake theory!
  • There’s a theory that pigeons are simply undercover agents—if they can handle the streets of New York, they can handle anything!

Cultural Notes

The term 'theory' is often used in both academic and casual contexts to explain hypotheses about the world. In science, a theory can become widely accepted if it is backed by substantial evidence.

verb

Definition

To form a theory or set of theories about something.

In ZH

形成理论或一组关于某事的理论。

Examples

  • Scientists theorize that coffee is the secret fuel behind every successful adult's life—it’s either that or sheer panic!
  • I used to theorize that socks disappear in the dryer because they were living their best lives in an alternate dimension. Turns out they probably just went to the sock Bermuda Triangle!
  • My friend theorizes that the reason we can't find the TV remote is because it has developed an existential crisis and is hiding from us.

Cultural Notes

This usage is more informal and is often used in everyday conversation when discussing ideas or assumptions.
