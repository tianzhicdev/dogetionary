Tranquility

Pronunciation:træŋˈkwɪl.ɪ.ti
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 宁静
  • 平静
  • 安宁

Definitions

noun

Definition

The state of being calm and peaceful; a quietness that brings relaxation and freedom from disturbance.

In ZH

一种宁静和平和的状态；一种带来放松和免受干扰的安静感。

Examples

  • During the pandemic, many people found tranquility in gardening, as their plants flourished while they struggled to manage their Zoom meetings.
  • I tried meditation for tranquility, but all I could think about was whether my pizza was ready.
  • The tranquility of the library was interrupted by someone who thought 'whispering' meant talking just a little less loudly than normal.

Cultural Notes

In many cultures, tranquility is associated with nature and mindfulness practices. It’s often sought after in settings like parks, gardens, or quiet areas to escape the chaos of urban life.

adjective

Definition

Characterized by an absence of disturbance or agitation; calm and serene in nature.

In ZH

以没有扰动或激动为特征；本质上平静和宁静。

Examples

  • The lake was so tranquil that I half-expected to see a swan in a tuxedo paddle by, looking for a five-star restaurant.
  • Watching the sunset by the tranquil beach, I forgot all my worries—until a seagull swooped down and stole my sandwich.
  • My cat has mastered the tranquil art of napping and breathes as if he's meditating, completely ignoring the chaos of my toddler.

Cultural Notes

Tranquil environments are often used as backdrops in art and literature to symbolize peace and introspection.

verb

Definition

To make or become tranquil; to calm and soothe.

In ZH

使或变得宁静；平静和安抚。

Examples

  • My friend tried to tranquil my anxiety by suggesting I binge-watch the latest reality show, but I ended up feeling more stressed by the drama.
  • After a long day, I tranquilized myself with a cup of herbal tea, only to be interrupted by my neighbor's loud karaoke session.
  • I attempted to tranquil the barking dog with classical music, but he seemed to prefer heavy metal instead.

Cultural Notes

The concept of tranquilization can also refer to medications or practices that calm feelings of anxiety or hyperactivity.
