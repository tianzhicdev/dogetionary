Transgressions

Pronunciation:trænsˈɡrɛʃənz
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 违反
  • 越界
  • 犯罪

Definitions

noun

Definition

An act that goes against a law, rule, or code of conduct; an offense.

In ZH

违反法律、规则或行为准则的行为；犯罪。

Examples

  • His transgressions were noted by the committee.
  • She was forgiven for her past transgressions.
  • The law was enacted to prevent future transgressions.

Cultural Notes

In many cultures, transgressions can lead to social stigma or legal consequences. The concept of transgression often relates to moral boundaries and societal expectations.

verb (transgress)

Definition

To violate a law, command, or moral code; to go beyond the limits set by a rule.

In ZH

违反法律、命令或道德规范；超越规则设定的界限。

Examples

  • He transgressed the boundaries of acceptable behavior.
  • They felt that breaking the silence would transgress their friendship's unspoken rules.
  • Transgressing the laws of nature can lead to disastrous consequences.

Cultural Notes

The verb 'transgress' is often used in formal contexts or philosophical discussions about morality and ethics. In literature, transgression can symbolize rebellion or a break from tradition.
