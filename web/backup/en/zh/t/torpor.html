Torpor

EN→ZH

What Does Torpor Mean?

  • 迟钝
  • 无精打采
  • 麻木

Torpor Pronunciation

/ˈtɔːrpər/

Torpor Definition

noun

Definition

A state of physical or mental inactivity; lethargy.

In ZH

一种身体或精神的不活动状态；无精打采。

How to Use Torpor in a Sentence

  • After the long meeting, there was a noticeable sense of torpor among the attendees.
  • The cold winter months often bring a sense of torpor to those living in the region.
  • She felt a torpor settle over her as she lounged on the couch all afternoon.
  • His torpor was evident as he struggled to stay awake during the lecture.
  • Torpor can sometimes be mistaken for a lack of interest in activities.

Synonyms for Torpor

  • Torpid
  • Torrid
