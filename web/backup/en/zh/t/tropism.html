Tropism

Pronunciation:/ˈtrōpɪzəm/
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 趋性
  • 向性

Definitions

noun

Definition

The turning or bending movement of an organism or a part of an organism toward or away from an external stimulus.

In ZH

生物或其部分朝向或远离外部刺激的转动或弯曲运动。

Examples

  • Plants exhibit phototropism by growing towards sunlight.
  • Roots typically show gravitropism, growing downward in response to gravity.
  • In the study of tropisms, we observe how animals respond to their environment.
  • Some flowers exhibit chemotropism, moving toward chemical signals.
  • Neurons can show electrical tropism, attracted to areas of higher electrical activity.
