Talisman

EN→ZH

What Does Talisman Mean?

  • 护身符
  • 吉祥物
  • 护符

Talisman Pronunciation

/ˈtælɪzmən/

Talisman Definition

noun

Definition

An object, typically inscribed with symbols or text, believed to have magical powers and able to bring good luck or protection.

In ZH

一种物品，通常刻有符号或文字，被认为具有魔法力量，能够带来好运或保护。

How to Use Talisman in a Sentence

  • He wore a talisman around his neck for good luck.
  • The ancient talisman was thought to ward off evil spirits.
  • She believed her talisman protected her during the journey.
  • Many cultures have their own versions of talismans for different purposes.
  • The museum displayed a collection of talismans from various civilizations.

Synonyms for Talisman

  • Talismanic
