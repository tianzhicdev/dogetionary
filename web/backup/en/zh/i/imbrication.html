Imbrication

Pronunciation:/ˌɪm.brɪˈkeɪ.ʃən/
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 重叠
  • 交错
  • 叠加

Definitions

noun

Definition

The arrangement or overlapping of layers, often used in geological and architectural contexts.

In ZH

层次的排列或重叠，常用于地质和建筑语境中。

Examples

  • The imbrication of roof tiles prevents water from leaking into the building.
  • Geologists studied the imbrication of rock layers to determine the area's formation history.
  • In architecture, the imbrication technique adds both aesthetic and functional value to structures.
  • The imbrication of leaves in the plant helps maximize sunlight absorption.
  • Understanding the imbrication of different species' habitats is critical for conservation efforts.
