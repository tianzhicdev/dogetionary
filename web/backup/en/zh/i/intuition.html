Intuition

Pronunciation:ɪnˈtjuːɪʃən
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 直觉
  • 直观
  • 直觉力

Definitions

noun

Definition

The ability to understand something instinctively, without the need for conscious reasoning.

In ZH

直觉是指一种本能的理解能力，无需意识推理。

Examples

  • She had an intuition that he was not telling the truth.
  • His intuition guided him to make the right choice.
  • Trust your intuition when making decisions.

Cultural Notes

In many cultures, intuition is considered a valuable trait, often linked to wisdom and emotional intelligence. It is sometimes seen as a 'gut feeling' or a natural understanding of a situation.

noun

Definition

A way of knowing something based on feelings rather than facts or analysis.

In ZH

直觉是一种基于感觉而非事实或分析的知晓方式。

Examples

  • Intuition often helps us make quick decisions in complex situations.
  • Her intuition about the market trends proved to be correct.
  • Relying on intuition can sometimes lead to unexpected outcomes.

Cultural Notes

In Western cultures, intuition is often respected as a source of insight, whereas in other cultures, analytical thinking may be prioritized over intuitive understanding.
