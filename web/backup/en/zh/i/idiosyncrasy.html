Idiosyncrasy

EN→ZH

What Does Idiosyncrasy Mean?

  • 特质
  • 个性特征
  • 习性

Idiosyncrasy Pronunciation

/ˌɪdiəˈsɪŋkrəsi/

Idiosyncrasy Definition

noun

Definition

A characteristic, habit, or mannerism that is peculiar to an individual.

In ZH

一个个体特有的特征、习惯或举止。

How to Use Idiosyncrasy in a Sentence

  • Her idiosyncrasy of always wearing mismatched socks made her unique.
  • He has a few idiosyncrasies that his friends find charming.
  • One idiosyncrasy of the artist is using everyday objects in his work.
  • Cultural idiosyncrasies can often lead to misunderstandings in communication.
  • Her idiosyncrasy of talking to plants is well known among her friends.
