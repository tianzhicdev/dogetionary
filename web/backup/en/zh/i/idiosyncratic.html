Idiosyncratic

EN→ZH

What Does Idiosyncratic Mean?

  • 特异的
  • 个人的
  • 独特的

Idiosyncratic Pronunciation

/ˌɪdiəʊsɪŋˈkrætɪk/

Idiosyncratic Definition

adjective

Definition

peculiar or individual; relating to idiosyncrasy.

In ZH

特有的或个人的；与特异性相关的。

How to Use Idiosyncratic in a Sentence

  • Her idiosyncratic taste in music surprised everyone at the party.
  • He has an idiosyncratic way of solving problems that often leads to innovative solutions.
  • The author's idiosyncratic style makes her work stand out in the literary world.
  • His idiosyncratic behavior raised eyebrows in the otherwise conventional office.
  • Each artist has their own idiosyncratic approach to their craft, making their work unique.

  • Idiomatic
  • Idiopathic
  • Idiosyncrasy
