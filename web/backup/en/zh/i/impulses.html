Impulses

Pronunciation:ˈɪm.pʌlsɪz
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 冲动
  • 脉冲
  • 驱动力

Definitions

noun

Definition

A sudden strong urge or desire to act, often without thinking.

In ZH

一种突然强烈的冲动或欲望，通常没有经过深思熟虑。

Examples

  • When I saw the last chocolate bar in the store, I had an impulse to buy it, even though I was on a diet.
  • After reading about the latest tech gadget, my impulse was to order it immediately, but my bank account disagreed!
  • Seeing the pizza delivery guy at the door gave me the impulse to forget my salad and embrace my true self.
noun

Definition

A physiological reaction that leads to a nerve action, such as a signal from the brain to the body.

In ZH

一种生理反应，导致神经动作，例如从大脑发出的信号到身体。

Examples

  • I felt an impulse to dance when I heard my favorite song, but my cat looked at me like I was crazy!
  • The doctor explained that our impulses can sometimes be our body's way of saying it's time to eat, sleep, or maybe just do a little jig!
  • After my coffee kicked in, I experienced an impulse to write a thousand-word essay on why my cat is the best roommate.
noun

Definition

A brief burst of energy or power, often associated with electrical or mechanical systems.

In ZH

一种短暂的能量或功率爆发，通常与电气或机械系统有关。

Examples

  • The car's engine emitted an impulse of sound that startled the squirrel crossing the road, making it rethink its life choices!
  • During the experiment, the scientist recorded an impulse that was so powerful it could light up a Christmas tree—if it weren't already July!
  • The light bulbs flickered with an impulse of power, reminding me that sometimes even technology needs a little vacation.
