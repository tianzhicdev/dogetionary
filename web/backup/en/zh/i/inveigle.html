Inveigle

EN→ZH

What Does Inveigle Mean?

  • 诱惑
  • 引诱
  • 哄骗

Inveigle Pronunciation

/ɪnˈveɪɡl/

Inveigle Definition

verb

Definition

to persuade someone to do something by means of deception or flattery

In ZH

通过欺骗或恭维说服某人做某事

How to Use Inveigle in a Sentence

  • She managed to inveigle him into signing the contract without reading it.
  • He was inveigled into attending the meeting under false pretenses.
  • The salesman's charm could easily inveigle unsuspecting customers.
  • They inveigled their way into the exclusive event by pretending to be VIP guests.
  • You shouldn't let anyone inveigle you into making hasty decisions.

Synonyms for Inveigle

  • Invective
  • Inveterate
  • Invigorate
  • Invincible
  • Inviolate
