Imputation

EN→ZH

What Does Imputation Mean?

  • 归因
  • 指控
  • 控告

Imputation Pronunciation

/ɪmˈpjuːteɪʃən/

Imputation Definition

noun

Definition

The act of attributing or assigning an action or quality to someone or something, often implying an unjust or unfounded accusation.

In ZH

将某个行为或特征归因于某人或某物的行为，通常暗示不公正或没有根据的指控。

How to Use Imputation in a Sentence

  • The imputation of motives can often be misleading.
  • Her imputation of guilt towards him was unfounded.
  • The report included an imputation of negligence on the part of the team.
  • He rejected the imputation that he was intentionally misleading the public.
  • Legal imputation can have serious consequences for an individual's reputation.

Synonyms for Imputation

  • Impact
  • Impasse
  • Impeccable
  • Impecuniosity
  • Impecunious
