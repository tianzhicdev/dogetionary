Buffer Zone

EN→ZH

What Does Buffer Zone Mean?

  • 缓冲区
  • 缓冲带
  • 隔离区

Buffer Zone Pronunciation

ˈbʌfər zoʊn

Buffer Zone Definition

noun

Definition

A buffer zone is an area that separates two or more areas, usually to reduce conflict, pollution, or other negative effects between them.

In ZH

缓冲区是指用来分隔两个或多个区域的地方，通常目的是减少它们之间的冲突、污染或其他负面影响。

How to Use Buffer Zone in a Sentence

  • The peace agreement established a buffer zone between the two countries to minimize future conflicts.
  • In urban planning, a buffer zone is created around parks to protect wildlife from the effects of nearby development.
  • The forest acts as a buffer zone for the lake, filtering pollutants before they reach the water.

Cultural Notes

Buffer zones are commonly used in environmental management and international relations. They reflect a strategic approach to conflict resolution and ecological preservation.

verb

Definition

To buffer (verb) means to act as a protective barrier or to mitigate the impact of something.

In ZH

缓冲（动词）意味着作为保护性屏障或减轻某种影响。

How to Use Buffer Zone in a Sentence

  • The organization worked to buffer the community from the negative impacts of industrial pollution.
  • We need to buffer our investment from market volatility by diversifying our portfolio.
  • The teacher used additional exercises to buffer students against the difficulties of the upcoming exams.

Cultural Notes

In a business context, buffering strategies are often discussed in risk management and strategic planning.
