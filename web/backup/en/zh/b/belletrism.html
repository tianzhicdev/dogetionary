Belletrism

EN→ZH

What Does Belletrism Mean?

  • 美文主义
  • 优美文学
  • 文学经典

Belletrism Pronunciation

/ˈbɛl.ə.trɪ.zəm/

Belletrism Definition

noun

Definition

A literary movement that emphasizes stylistic and aesthetic qualities over the practical or thematic content of literature.

In ZH

一种文学运动，强调文学的风格和美学特征，而非其实际内容或主题。

How to Use Belletrism in a Sentence

  • Belletrism has often influenced how literature is critiqued.
  • Many writers in the 19th century adhered to the principles of belletrism.
  • The focus on beauty and form in belletrism can sometimes overshadow the narrative.
  • Critics argue that belletrism may neglect the social responsibilities of literature.
  • In academic circles, belletrism is often discussed in relation to romanticism.

Cultural Notes

Belletrism emerged as a response to the growing emphasis on realism and naturalism in literature, advocating for a return to beauty and artistic expression.

Synonyms for Belletrism

  • Belletrist
  • Belletristic
  • Bellicose
  • Bellicosity
  • Belligerent
