Bromide

EN→ZH

What Does Bromide Mean?

  • 溴化物
  • 庸俗的言论

Bromide Pronunciation

/ˈbroʊmaɪd/

Bromide Definition

noun

Definition

A compound formed by the element bromine and another element or radical, commonly used in medicine and photography.

In ZH

由溴和其他元素或基团形成的化合物，常用于医学和摄影。

How to Use Bromide in a Sentence

  • The pharmacist dispensed a bromide for the patient's condition.
  • Bromides are often used as sedatives.
  • In photography, some films contain bromides to enhance image quality.
  • The use of potassium bromide has declined with the advent of more effective treatments.
  • A bromide is also a phrase that is overly common or dull.
noun

Definition

A bland or trite statement that is intended to soothe or placate but is ultimately unhelpful.

In ZH

一种平淡或陈腐的陈述，旨在安抚或缓解但最终无助。

How to Use Bromide in a Sentence

  • His speech was filled with bromides that failed to inspire the audience.
  • Saying 'everything will be fine' is just a bromide when faced with real problems.
  • People often resort to bromides during difficult conversations.
  • She smiled and offered a bromide to lift the mood.
  • In times of crisis, politicians often use bromides to calm the public.

Cultural Notes

The term 'bromide' in the context of language often refers to statements that lack originality or depth.

Synonyms for Bromide

  • Brobdingnagian
  • Brocade
  • Brogue
  • Bromidic
