Blasé

EN→ZH

What Does Blasé Mean?

  • 无所谓的
  • 冷漠的
  • 厌倦的

Blasé Pronunciation

/blɑːˈzeɪ/

Blasé Definition

adjective

Definition

unimpressed or indifferent to something because one has seen it so many times before.

In ZH

因为看过太多次而对某事毫不印象或漠不关心。

How to Use Blasé in a Sentence

  • After attending so many art galleries, he became quite blasé about the whole experience.
  • She felt blasé about the extravagant parties that everyone else seemed to enjoy.
  • He was so blasé about his job that he barely noticed the accolades he received.
  • The critics were often described as blasé, having seen too many similar performances.
  • Living in the city made her feel blasé about urban life, as everything seemed the same.

Synonyms for Blasé

  • Blandishment
  • Blandishments
  • Blarney
  • Blatherskite
