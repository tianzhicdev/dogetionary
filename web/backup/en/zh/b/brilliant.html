Brilliant

Pronunciation:ˈbrɪljənt
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 杰出的
  • 辉煌的
  • 聪明的

Definitions

adjective

Definition

Very bright or radiant, reflecting a high level of light.

In ZH

非常明亮或辐射的，反射出高水平的光。

Examples

  • The stars were brilliant against the dark night sky.
  • She wore a brilliant red dress to the party.
  • The brilliant sunlight made the flowers look even more colorful.

Cultural Notes

In visual contexts, 'brilliant' often describes vibrant colors or intense light.

adjective

Definition

Exceptional or outstanding in a particular field; highly intelligent or talented.

In ZH

在特定领域中表现出色或杰出；非常聪明或有才能。

Examples

  • He is a brilliant scientist known for his groundbreaking research.
  • Her brilliant ideas helped improve the project significantly.
  • The performance was brilliant and received a standing ovation.

Cultural Notes

This usage often applies to academics, creativity, and talent.

adjective

Definition

Extremely clever or skillful in a way that is impressive.

In ZH

以令人印象深刻的方式极其聪明或技艺高超。

Examples

  • The brilliant strategy led to their victory in the championship.
  • Her brilliant solution to the problem amazed everyone.
  • He came up with a brilliant way to save money.

Cultural Notes

Often used to praise someone's ingenuity or quick thinking.
