Brabble

EN→ZH

What Does Brabble Mean?

  • 争论
  • 争吵
  • 喋喋不休

Brabble Pronunciation

/ˈbræb.əl/

Brabble Definition

verb

Definition

to argue or quarrel noisily; to talk foolishly or nonsensically

In ZH

吵闹地争论或争吵；愚蠢或无意义地交谈

How to Use Brabble in a Sentence

  • They began to brabble over trivial matters.
  • The children brabbled about who would get to use the toy first.
  • During the meeting, some members brabbled instead of discussing serious issues.
  • She couldn't concentrate because they were brabbling in the other room.
  • It's pointless to brabble when we should be finding a solution.

Cultural Notes

The term is often used in a somewhat literary or archaic context.

noun

Definition

a noisy argument or quarrel

In ZH

吵闹的争论或争吵

How to Use Brabble in a Sentence

  • The brabble between the two neighbors went on for hours.
  • Their constant brabble made it difficult to enjoy the peaceful surroundings.
  • After the brabble, they decided it was better to agree to disagree.
  • He was tired of the brabble and just wanted some peace.
  • The brabble ended when someone finally stepped in to mediate.

Cultural Notes

Brabble as a noun is less commonly used in modern English and may be considered somewhat quaint.

Synonyms for Brabble

  • Bra
  • Brachiation
  • Braggadocio
  • Braggadocious
  • Braggart
