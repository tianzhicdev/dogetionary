Bilocation

EN→ZH

What Does Bilocation Mean?

  • 双重定位
  • 双重存在
  • 双重影像

Bilocation Pronunciation

/ˌbaɪloʊˈkeɪʃən/

Bilocation Definition

noun

Definition

The ability to be in two places at the same time; a phenomenon associated with certain religious or mystical figures.

In ZH

同时出现在两个地方的能力；与某些宗教或神秘人物相关的现象。

How to Use Bilocation in a Sentence

  • Some mystics claim to have experienced bilocation during deep meditation.
  • The legend of the saint included stories of his bilocation.
  • During the seance, the medium spoke of bilocation as a common occurrence.
  • Bilocation is a concept often found in religious texts.
  • Research into bilocation leads to fascinating discussions about consciousness.

Cultural Notes

Bilocation is often discussed in the context of mysticism and religious experiences, particularly in Christianity where saints are said to exhibit this phenomenon.

Synonyms for Bilocation

  • Bilious
  • Billet-Doux
