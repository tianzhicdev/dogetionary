Branches Of Microbiology

EN→ZH

What Does Branches Of Microbiology Mean?

  • 微生物学分支

Branches Of Microbiology Pronunciation

ˈbrænʧɪz ʌv maɪkroʊˈbaɪəloʊdʒi

Branches Of Microbiology Definition

noun

Definition

The various specialized fields within microbiology, including areas such as bacteriology, virology, mycology, and parasitology.

In ZH

微生物学中的各个专业领域，包括细菌学、病毒学、真菌学和寄生虫学等。

How to Use Branches Of Microbiology in a Sentence

  • Bacteriology is one of the key branches of microbiology that focuses on the study of bacteria.
  • Virology, another branch of microbiology, investigates viruses and their effects on living organisms.
  • Mycology is concerned with the study of fungi, which is another important branch of microbiology.

Cultural Notes

In academic settings, understanding the branches of microbiology is essential for students pursuing a career in life sciences. Each branch has its own set of techniques and applications.

noun

Definition

A subdivision of the broader field of microbiology that concentrates on a specific type of microorganism or aspect of microbiological research.

In ZH

微生物学这个更广泛领域的一个细分，专注于某种特定类型的微生物或微生物研究的某一方面。

How to Use Branches Of Microbiology in a Sentence

  • The study of pathogenic microorganisms falls under the branch of medical microbiology.
  • Environmental microbiology is a branch that explores the role of microbes in ecosystems.
  • Food microbiology examines the microorganisms involved in food production and spoilage.

Cultural Notes

Different branches of microbiology can have significant implications for public health, agriculture, and environmental science.
