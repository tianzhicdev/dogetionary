Bandersnatch

EN→ZH

What Does Bandersnatch Mean?

  • 巴德斯纳奇
  • 带尔斯纳奇

Bandersnatch Pronunciation

/ˈbændərsnæʧ/

Bandersnatch Definition

noun

Definition

A fictional creature from Lewis Carroll's poem 'The Hunting of the Snark', known for its wild, unpredictable behavior.

In ZH

路易斯·卡罗尔的诗《猎觅斯纳克》中的虚构生物，以其狂野和不可预测的行为而闻名。

How to Use Bandersnatch in a Sentence

  • In Lewis Carroll's poem, the bandersnatch is described as a fearsome beast.
  • Children love to hear stories about the mischievous bandersnatch.
  • The bandersnatch symbolizes the chaotic nature of life.
  • When he encountered the bandersnatch, he felt both fear and excitement.
  • The concept of the bandersnatch has been referenced in various works of literature.

Cultural Notes

The bandersnatch has become a cultural reference in discussions about the unpredictable and chaotic aspects of life.
