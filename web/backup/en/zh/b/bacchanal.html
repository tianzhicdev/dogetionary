Bacchanal

EN→ZH

What Does Bacchanal Mean?

  • 狂欢节
  • 酒神节
  • 盛宴

Bacchanal Pronunciation

/ˈbækənæl/

Bacchanal Definition

noun

Definition

A revelry or party characterized by drunkenness and uninhibited behavior.

In ZH

一种以醉酒和无拘无束的行为为特征的狂欢或聚会。

How to Use Bacchanal in a Sentence

  • The annual bacchanal attracted people from all over the city.
  • During the festival, the streets turned into a bacchanal of music and dancing.
  • We joined the bacchanal to celebrate the harvest season.
  • The invitation promised a night of bacchanal and excess.
  • There was a sense of freedom in the air during the bacchanal.
noun

Definition

A celebration or festival in honor of Bacchus, the Roman god of wine.

In ZH

为罗马酒神巴克斯而举行的庆祝活动或节日。

How to Use Bacchanal in a Sentence

  • The ancient bacchanal was filled with wine and laughter.
  • Many people attend the bacchanal to worship the god of wine.
  • The history of the bacchanal dates back to ancient Rome.
  • In the bacchanal, participants often wore masks and costumes.
  • The celebration turned into a bacchanal, with everyone enjoying the festivities.

Synonyms for Bacchanal

  • Baccate
  • Bacchanalia
  • Backpeddle
  • Bacterium
