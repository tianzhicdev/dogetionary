Corybantic

EN→ZH

What Does Corybantic Mean?

  • 狂野的
  • 狂热的
  • 喧嚣的

Corybantic Pronunciation

/kɔːrɪˈbæntɪk/

Corybantic Definition

adjective

Definition

Relating to wild, frenzied, or unrestrained behavior; often associated with ecstatic dancing or rites in honor of the goddess Cybele.

In ZH

与狂野、喧闹或不受约束的行为有关；通常与为女神基贝尔（Cybele）而举行的狂欢舞蹈或仪式有关。

How to Use Corybantic in a Sentence

  • The corybantic atmosphere at the festival was contagious.
  • He danced in a corybantic manner, losing himself in the rhythm.
  • The performance included corybantic movements that captivated the audience.
  • Corybantic celebrations filled the streets during the carnival.
  • Her corybantic laughter echoed through the halls.

Cultural Notes

The term corybantic is derived from the Corybantes, who were priest-like figures in ancient Phrygia known for their ecstatic and frenzied worship of the goddess Cybele.
