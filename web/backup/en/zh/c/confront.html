Confront

EN→ZH

What Does Confront Mean?

  • 面对
  • 对抗
  • 冲突

Confront Pronunciation

kənˈfrʌnt

Confront Definition

verb

Definition

to face someone or something in a challenging way, often regarding a difficult situation.

In ZH

以具有挑战性的方式面对某人或某事，通常涉及困难的情况。

How to Use Confront in a Sentence

  • She decided to confront her fears and speak in public.
  • We must confront the issue of climate change urgently.
  • He confronted his opponent in the debate with strong arguments.

Cultural Notes

In many cultures, confronting someone can be seen as rude or aggressive, but in others, it is considered a necessary part of resolving conflicts.

noun

Definition

the act of confronting; a situation where you face a problem directly.

In ZH

面对的行为；直接面对问题的情况。

How to Use Confront in a Sentence

  • The confrontation between the two leaders was highly publicized.
  • Her confrontation with the bully was brave and inspiring.
  • The meeting ended in a confrontation about the project deadlines.

Cultural Notes

Confrontations are common in discussions about social issues in various cultures, reflecting different approaches to communication and conflict resolution.

Synonyms for Confront

  • Concatenation
  • Concept
  • Concomitant
  • Concordant
