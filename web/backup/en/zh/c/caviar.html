Caviar

Pronunciation:ˈkæviˌɑr
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 鱼子酱

Definitions

noun

Definition

Caviar is a luxury food consisting of the salted eggs (roe) of sturgeon and some other fish. It is often served as a delicacy and is considered a gourmet item in various cuisines.

In ZH

鱼子酱是由鲟鱼和其他一些鱼类的盐腌鱼卵组成的奢侈食品。它通常作为美食呈现，被视为各种菜肴中的美味食材。

Examples

  • Caviar is often served on blinis as an appetizer.
  • Many people consider caviar to be a symbol of luxury and wealth.
  • She ordered caviar for her birthday dinner, treating herself to something special.

Cultural Notes

Caviar has a long history of being associated with wealth and high status. In many cultures, it is traditionally served at celebrations and formal occasions.
