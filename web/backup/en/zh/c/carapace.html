Carapace

Pronunciation:/ˈkær.ə.pes/
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 甲壳
  • 外壳
  • 盾甲

Definitions

noun

Definition

A hard shell that covers and protects the back of certain animals, such as turtles and crustaceans.

In ZH

覆盖并保护某些动物（如乌龟和甲壳类动物）背部的硬壳。

Examples

  • The turtle retreated into its carapace when it sensed danger.
  • Crabs have a strong carapace that protects them from predators.
  • The ancient fossil showed the carapace of a prehistoric turtle.
  • Some species of beetles have a colorful carapace that helps them blend into their environment.
  • The carapace of a lobster is tough and helps it survive in the wild.
