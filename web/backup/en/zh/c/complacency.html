Complacency

EN→ZH

What Does Complacency Mean?

  • 自满
  • 满足
  • 得意忘形

Complacency Pronunciation

/kəmˈpleɪsənsi/

Complacency Definition

noun

Definition

A feeling of smug or uncritical satisfaction with oneself or one's achievements.

In ZH

对自己或自己成就的自满或无批判的满意感。

How to Use Complacency in a Sentence

  • His complacency about the project led to many overlooked details.
  • She showed complacency after winning the award, believing she was invincible.
  • The team's complacency resulted in a lack of preparation for the final match.
  • Complacency can prevent growth and improvement in personal development.
  • The manager warned against complacency in the workplace, urging everyone to strive for excellence.

Synonyms for Complacency

  • Comely
  • Comestible
  • Comity
  • Commensal
  • Commensurate
