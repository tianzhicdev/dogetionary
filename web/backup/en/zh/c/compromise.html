Compromise

Pronunciation:ˈkɒmprəmaɪz
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 妥协
  • 折中方案
  • 妥协方案

Definitions

noun

Definition

An agreement or a settlement of a dispute that is reached by each side making concessions.

In ZH

一种通过双方让步而达成的协议或争议解决方案。

Examples

  • After several hours of negotiation, they finally reached a compromise that satisfied both parties.
  • In a compromise, both individuals may have to give up something to move forward.
  • The peace treaty was a compromise that helped end the long-standing conflict.

Cultural Notes

Compromise is often seen as a strength in many cultures, as it demonstrates the ability to work together and find common ground.

verb

Definition

To make a settlement or agreement by mutual concession.

In ZH

通过相互让步达成妥协或协议。

Examples

  • Sometimes, you have to compromise your own desires for the sake of your team.
  • In relationships, it’s important to compromise in order to maintain harmony.
  • He refused to compromise on his principles, even if it meant losing the deal.

Cultural Notes

In many Western cultures, the ability to compromise is viewed positively, indicating flexibility and willingness to collaborate.
