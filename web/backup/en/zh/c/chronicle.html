Chronicle

Pronunciation:/ˈkrɒnɪkəl/
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 编年史
  • 年代记
  • 历史
  • 记录

Definitions

noun

Definition

A factual written account of important or historical events in the order of their occurrence.

In ZH

按发生顺序记录的重要或历史事件的事实书面帐户。

Examples

  • The chronicle of the town's history was published last year.
  • She wrote a chronicle of her travels throughout Europe.
  • In the library, I found a chronicle detailing the events of the revolution.
  • The historian spent years compiling the local chronicle.
  • His chronicle of the war was both insightful and detailed.
verb

Definition

To record events in a factual and detailed way.

In ZH

以事实和详细的方式记录事件。

Examples

  • The reporter will chronicle the unfolding story as it develops.
  • They chronicle their daily lives in a shared journal.
  • The documentary aims to chronicle the impact of climate change.
  • Historians chronicle the lives of famous figures for future generations.
  • She decided to chronicle her experiences during the pandemic.
