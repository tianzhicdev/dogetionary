Cassock

EN→ZH

What Does Cassock Mean?

  • 法衣
  • 神父袍
  • 长袍

Cassock Pronunciation

/ˈkæs.ək/

Cassock Definition

noun

Definition

A long, close-fitting garment worn by clergymen or in certain religious orders.

In ZH

一种由神职人员或某些宗教团体穿着的长而贴身的服装。

How to Use Cassock in a Sentence

  • The priest wore a black cassock during the ceremony.
  • In some traditions, clergymen are required to wear a cassock.
  • The cassock is often associated with formal religious attire.
  • He adjusted his cassock before entering the church.
  • Many bishops are seen in a cassock at official events.

Synonyms for Cassock

  • Casualist
