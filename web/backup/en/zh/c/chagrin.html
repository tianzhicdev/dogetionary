Chagrin

EN→ZH

What Does Chagrin Mean?

  • 懊恼
  • 气恼
  • 失望

Chagrin Pronunciation

/ʃəˈɡrɪn/

Chagrin Definition

noun

Definition

A feeling of distress or embarrassment at having failed or been humiliated.

In ZH

因失败或被羞辱而感到的痛苦或尴尬。

How to Use Chagrin in a Sentence

  • She felt a deep sense of chagrin after forgetting her lines on stage.
  • To his chagrin, he discovered that he had lost the key.
  • Her chagrin was evident when she realized her mistake in the report.
  • He tried to hide his chagrin when he missed the opportunity.
  • Much to my chagrin, I realized I had missed the deadline.

Synonyms for Chagrin

  • Chalcedony
  • Challenge
  • Change
  • Channel
  • Chaos
