Crystal

Pronunciation:ˈkrɪs.təl
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 水晶
  • 晶体
  • 结晶

Definitions

noun

Definition

A clear, transparent mineral or rock that has a regular geometric form.

In ZH

一种透明的矿物或岩石，具有规则的几何形状。

Examples

  • The crystal was beautifully cut and sparkled in the sunlight.
  • She collected crystal figurines to display in her living room.
  • The geologist examined the crystal structure of the mineral.

Cultural Notes

Crystals are often valued not only for their beauty but also in various cultures for their supposed healing properties.

verb

Definition

To form crystals or to cause something to form crystals.

In ZH

形成晶体或使某物形成晶体。

Examples

  • The sugar began to crystalize as it cooled.
  • With the right conditions, salt can easily crystalize from seawater.
  • She watched the ice crystalize on the window overnight.

Cultural Notes

This usage is often seen in scientific contexts, particularly in chemistry and geology.

adjective

Definition

Clear, transparent, or resembling a crystal in clarity.

In ZH

清晰、透明，或在清晰度上类似于水晶。

Examples

  • The water was crystal clear, making it easy to see the fish below.
  • He had crystal blue eyes that sparkled with enthusiasm.
  • The instructions were crystal clear, leaving no room for confusion.

Cultural Notes

This term is often used metaphorically to describe something that is very clear or easy to understand.
