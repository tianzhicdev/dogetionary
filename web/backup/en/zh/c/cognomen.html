Cognomen

Pronunciation:/kɒɡˈnoʊ.mən/
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 姓
  • 外号
  • 名号

Definitions

noun

Definition

A personal name or nickname, especially one indicating lineage or clan.

In ZH

个人名字或外号，尤其指示血统或家族的名字。

Examples

  • In ancient Rome, a cognomen was often used to distinguish between branches of a family.
  • His cognomen was derived from his father's profession as a blacksmith.
  • Many people have a cognomen that reflects their heritage or ancestry.
  • In some cultures, a cognomen can be a source of pride or identity.
  • She chose a cognomen that honors her family's traditions.

Cultural Notes

In Roman culture, the cognomen was the third name in the tria nomina system, used to identify families.
