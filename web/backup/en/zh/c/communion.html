Communion

Pronunciation:kəˈmjuːn.jən
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 圣餐
  • 交往
  • 共享

Definitions

noun

Definition

The act of sharing or exchanging thoughts, feelings, or experiences with others.

In ZH

与他人分享或交流思想、感情或经历的行为。

Examples

  • After everyone shared their funny quarantine stories, it felt like a communion of laughter.
  • The book club's communion over the latest bestseller turned into a debate about which character would win in a baking competition.
  • At the online meeting, the communion of awkward silences was strong, especially when everyone was on mute.

Cultural Notes

In social contexts, 'communion' often refers to a deep, shared understanding among friends or colleagues, akin to having a heart-to-heart conversation.

noun

Definition

A religious ceremony in which bread and wine are consumed as a representation of sharing in the body and blood of Christ, particularly in Christian traditions.

In ZH

一种宗教仪式，其中食用面包和酒，作为共享基督的身体和鲜血的象征，特别是在基督教传统中。

Examples

  • During the service, the priest made an awkward joke about the gluten-free communion bread.
  • After a long debate, they finally decided that grape juice would be the official communion drink for the youth group.
  • The church put out a Facebook poll asking if it should serve wine or sparkling grape juice for communion, leading to an intense discussion about wine pairings.

Cultural Notes

In many Christian denominations, communion is a significant ritual symbolizing unity and remembrance of Jesus Christ. The ceremony varies greatly among different faiths.
