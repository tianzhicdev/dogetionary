Cormorant

EN→ZH

What Does Cormorant Mean?

  • 鸬鹚
  • 鸬鹚鸟

Cormorant Pronunciation

/ˈkɔːrmərənt/

Cormorant Definition

noun

Definition

A large diving bird with a long neck and a hooked bill, known for its fishing ability.

In ZH

一种大型潜水鸟，颈部较长，嘴钩状，以捕鱼能力而闻名。

How to Use Cormorant in a Sentence

  • The cormorant dove underwater to catch a fish.
  • We spotted a cormorant resting on the rock by the lake.
  • The fisherman watched as a cormorant caught its prey.
  • Cormorants are often seen near coastal areas.
  • Many cultures have used trained cormorants to fish.

Synonyms for Cormorant

  • Cordon
  • Cornucopia
  • Corollary
  • Corporeal
