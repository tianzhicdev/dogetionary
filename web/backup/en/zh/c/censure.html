Censure

EN→ZH

What Does Censure Mean?

  • 谴责
  • 指责
  • 批评

Censure Pronunciation

/ˈsɛnʃər/

Censure Definition

noun

Definition

The expression of formal disapproval.

In ZH

正式的不赞成表达。

How to Use Censure in a Sentence

  • The council's censure of the mayor was unexpected.
  • Censure from peers can severely impact one's career.
  • The senator faced censure for his inappropriate remarks.
  • After the censure, he decided to resign from his position.
  • Public censure can often lead to a loss of reputation.
verb

Definition

To express severe disapproval of someone or something, typically in a formal statement.

In ZH

以正式声明表达对某人或某事的严重不赞成。

How to Use Censure in a Sentence

  • The committee voted to censure the member for violating rules.
  • He was censured for his unprofessional behavior.
  • Censuring public officials is a way to hold them accountable.
  • The board decided to censure the employee for misconduct.
  • Many lawmakers are subject to censure for ethical violations.

Synonyms for Censure

  • Cenotaph
  • Censures
