Cynicism

EN→ZH

What Does Cynicism Mean?

  • 愤世嫉俗
  • 玩世不恭

Cynicism Pronunciation

ˈsɪnɪˌsɪzəm

Cynicism Definition

noun

Definition

An inclination to believe that people are motivated purely by self-interest; skepticism regarding the integrity or altruism of others.

In ZH

倾向于相信人们的动机完全是出于自私的利益；对他人的诚实或利他主义持怀疑态度。

How to Use Cynicism in a Sentence

  • Her cynicism about politics makes it hard for her to trust any politician.
  • After years of disappointment, he developed a deep cynicism towards love and relationships.
  • The author's cynicism is evident in his portrayal of the corporate world.

Cultural Notes

Cynicism can be seen both positively and negatively in different cultures. In Western settings, it often relates to a healthy skepticism, while in some Eastern cultures, it may be viewed more negatively as a lack of faith in societal norms.

adjective

Definition

Of or relating to an attitude of cynicism; cynical.

In ZH

与愤世嫉俗的态度有关；愤世嫉俗的。

How to Use Cynicism in a Sentence

  • He made a cynical remark about the charity event, questioning the motives behind it.
  • Her cynical view on friendship made it difficult for her to form close connections.
  • The film presents a cynical take on the idea of the American dream.

Cultural Notes

Cynical attitudes can influence social interactions and relationships. In some cultures, being too cynical may be considered rude or negative, while in others, it may be accepted as a form of realism.

Synonyms for Cynicism

  • Cynosural
  • Cynosure
