Caper

Pronunciation:/ˈkeɪ.pər/
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 愚蠢的行为
  • 调皮捣蛋
  • 小恶作剧
  • 小偷行为

Definitions

noun

Definition

A playful leap or skip; a prank or mischievous act.

In ZH

一种顽皮的跳跃或跳步；恶作剧或调皮的行为。

Examples

  • The children did a little caper in the park.
  • He pulled off a caper that left everyone laughing.
  • They planned a caper to steal the last piece of cake.
  • Her joyful capers brought smiles to everyone's faces.
  • The movie features a clever caper that keeps viewers guessing.

Cultural Notes

In various contexts, 'caper' can also refer to an exciting adventure or scheme, often associated with playful mischief.

verb

Definition

To leap or skip playfully; to frolic.

In ZH

顽皮地跳跃或跳步；嬉戏。

Examples

  • The puppy capered around the yard.
  • She capered through the meadow, filled with joy.
  • They capered along the beach, enjoying the sunshine.
  • He capered like a child at the fair.
  • The kids capered while waiting for the parade.
