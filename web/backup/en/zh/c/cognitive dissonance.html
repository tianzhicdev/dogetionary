Cognitive Dissonance

EN→ZH

What Does Cognitive Dissonance Mean?

  • 认知失调
  • 认知不和谐

Cognitive Dissonance Pronunciation

/ˈkɒɡnɪtɪv ˈdɪsənəns/

Cognitive Dissonance Definition

noun

Definition

A psychological phenomenon that occurs when a person experiences conflicting beliefs, attitudes, or behaviors, leading to feelings of discomfort and tension.

In ZH

当一个人经历信念、态度或行为之间的冲突时，会产生一种心理现象，导致不适和紧张的感觉。

How to Use Cognitive Dissonance in a Sentence

  • Many smokers experience cognitive dissonance because they know smoking is harmful to their health.
  • After the debate, she felt cognitive dissonance about her political beliefs.
  • When he bought a new car, he faced cognitive dissonance about his need for a more eco-friendly vehicle.
  • Cognitive dissonance can lead people to change their beliefs to match their actions.
  • Therapists often address cognitive dissonance to help clients resolve internal conflicts.

Synonyms for Cognitive Dissonance

  • Cogency
  • Cogent
  • Cogitate
  • Cogitation
  • Cogitative
