Cavorting

Pronunciation:/kəˈvɔːrtɪŋ/
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 嬉戏
  • 欢跃
  • 嬉闹

Definitions

verb

Definition

To jump or dance around excitedly.

In ZH

欢快地跳跃或舞动。

Examples

  • The children were cavorting in the park.
  • They spent the afternoon cavorting on the beach.
  • The puppies were cavorting around their mother.
  • At the festival, people were cavorting joyfully under the lights.
  • The dancers were cavorting together in perfect harmony.
verb

Definition

To engage in lively or boisterous activities, often implying playful or carefree behavior.

In ZH

参与活泼或喧闹的活动，通常暗示调皮或无忧无虑的行为。

Examples

  • During the celebration, everyone was cavorting and laughing.
  • The animals were cavorting in the meadow during the spring.
  • Young adults were seen cavorting at the rooftop party.
  • At the reunion, old friends were cavorting like they were kids again.
  • The mascots were cavorting around the field, entertaining the crowd.
