Catachresis

Pronunciation:/ˌkætəˈkriːsɪs/
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 滥用词语
  • 错用法

Definitions

noun

Definition

A misuse of a word, especially when used in a context that it does not appropriately belong to.

In ZH

一种词语的误用，尤其是当在不适合的语境中使用时。

Examples

  • He used catachresis when he said, 'The knife sang as it cut through the apple.'
  • Many poets intentionally use catachresis to evoke strong imagery.
  • The phrase 'blind mouths' in his poem is a classic example of catachresis.
  • Catachresis can sometimes lead to confusion in communication.
  • In his speech, he demonstrated catachresis by referring to a 'deafening silence'.

Cultural Notes

Catachresis is often seen in literature and rhetoric as a stylistic device, but it can also reflect a lack of precision in language.
