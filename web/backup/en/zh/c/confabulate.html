Confabulate

Pronunciation:/kənˈfæbjʊleɪt/
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 交谈
  • 闲聊
  • 虚构

Definitions

verb

Definition

To engage in conversation; to chat or talk informally.

In ZH

进行交谈；非正式地聊天或谈话。

Examples

  • During dinner, we confabulated about our summer plans.
  • They often confabulate at the coffee shop after work.
  • The two old friends confabulated for hours, reminiscing about their school days.
  • At the conference, participants confabulated between sessions to share ideas.
  • The kids confabulated excitedly about their upcoming field trip.
verb

Definition

To fabricate or create false memories without intending to deceive, often seen in psychological contexts.

In ZH

无意中创造或编造虚假记忆，通常在心理学背景中看到。

Examples

  • Patients may confabulate when asked about events they can't remember.
  • He began to confabulate about his childhood, mixing real memories with fantasies.
  • In therapy, confabulation can be a way to fill memory gaps.
  • She confabulated details of her past to make her story more interesting.
  • Confabulation is often studied in cases of brain injury.

Cultural Notes

In psychology, confabulation can be a significant symptom in conditions like Korsakoff syndrome.
