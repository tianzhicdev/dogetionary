Jotun

Pronunciation:/ˈjɒtʊn/
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 巨人
  • 雅顿

Definitions

noun

Definition

In Norse mythology, a jotun is a giant and a member of the race of beings opposed to the gods (Aesir and Vanir).

In ZH

在北欧神话中，jotun是巨人，是与神（阿萨神族和芬族）对立的生物种族的成员。

Examples

  • The jotun are often depicted as formidable opponents of the gods.
  • In the legends, the jotun had incredible strength and size.
  • Many stories revolve around the battles between the jotun and the Aesir.
  • Jotuns are said to reside in Jotunheim, a realm of ice and mountains.
  • The concept of jotun has influenced many modern fantasy works.

Cultural Notes

Jotun are significant figures in Norse mythology, symbolizing chaos and nature, often representing the untamed forces of the world.
