Fulminate

EN→ZH

What Does Fulminate Mean?

  • 强烈抗议
  • 爆发
  • 猛烈抨击

Fulminate Pronunciation

/ˈfʌlmɪneɪt/

Fulminate Definition

verb

Definition

to issue an explosive verbal attack or denunciation.

In ZH

发出激烈的口头攻击或谴责。

How to Use Fulminate in a Sentence

  • The senator fulminated against the proposed budget cuts.
  • She fulminated about the injustice she had witnessed.
  • Many students fulminated over the new school policies.
  • He fulminated in his speech, condemning the government's actions.
  • The critics fulminated against the movie's portrayal of historical events.
verb

Definition

to explode with a loud noise; to detonate.

In ZH

发出巨响爆炸；引爆。

How to Use Fulminate in a Sentence

  • The fireworks fulminated in the night sky, creating a stunning display.
  • The bomb fulminated, causing widespread damage to the area.
  • They watched in awe as the volcano fulminated in the distance.
  • The gas had built up to a point where it could fulminate at any moment.
  • When the fuel ignited, it fulminated with a blinding flash.

Synonyms for Fulminate

  • Fulgent
  • Fuliginous
  • Full
