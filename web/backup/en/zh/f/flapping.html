Flapping

Pronunciation:ˈflæpɪŋ
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 拍打
  • 振动

Definitions

verb

Definition

The act of moving one’s wings or other body parts up and down quickly.

In ZH

快速上下移动翅膀或其他身体部位的动作。

Examples

  • The bird was flapping its wings so hard that it looked like it was trying to take off in a tornado!
  • When I saw the chicken flapping around at the farm, I questioned if it was a bird or just auditioning for the next dance-off!
  • I tried to impress my friends by flapping my arms like a bird, but they just laughed and suggested I stick to walking.

Cultural Notes

In many cultures, the image of flapping usually brings to mind birds, but in a humorous context, it can also be associated with awkward movements or attempts at flight that end in failure.

noun

Definition

A type of movement characterized by rapid and irregular motion, often used to describe the motion of birds’ wings.

In ZH

一种特征为快速且不规则运动的运动类型，通常用来描述鸟翅膀的动作。

Examples

  • The flapping of the ducks by the lake was so loud, it sounded like they were holding a meeting about bread crumbs!
  • At the summer festival, the flapping of the colorful kites almost stole the show from the singers on stage!
  • During the parade, the flapping banners made it seem like they were cheering louder than the crowd itself.

Cultural Notes

The term ‘flapping’ can also reflect lively scenarios in festivals and parades, where flags and banners are waved about energetically.

adjective

Definition

Describing a sound or motion that is quick, light, and often erratic, similar to the way wings move when flapping.

In ZH

描述快速、轻盈且常常不规则的声音或动作，类似于翅膀拍打时的运动。

Examples

  • The flapping sounds coming from the kitchen made me think my dinner was trying to escape!
  • Every time my neighbor's cat saw a butterfly, it made the flapping noise that could wake up the entire neighborhood!
  • I thought it was raining, but it was just my friend trying to keep his flapping umbrella in control against the wind.

Cultural Notes

In music and performances, flapping sounds can add rhythm and energy, making them memorable parts of a show.
