Feeble

EN→ZH

What Does Feeble Mean?

  • 虚弱的
  • 微弱的
  • 薄弱的

Feeble Pronunciation

ˈfiː.bəl

Feeble Definition

adjective

Definition

lacking physical strength, especially as a result of age or illness

In ZH

缺乏体力，尤其是由于年龄或疾病导致的

How to Use Feeble in a Sentence

  • After the surgery, he felt too feeble to get out of bed.
  • The feeble old man struggled to carry his grocery bags.
  • Her feeble attempts at exercise were not enough to improve her health.

Cultural Notes

In many cultures, the idea of being feeble can be associated with vulnerability, which might elicit feelings of compassion or protection from others. However, it's important to be sensitive when describing someone as feeble, as it can carry negative connotations.

adjective

Definition

lacking in force or effectiveness

In ZH

缺乏力量或效果的

How to Use Feeble in a Sentence

  • The argument he presented was feeble and did not convince anyone.
  • Her feeble efforts to persuade him fell flat.
  • The team's feeble performance during the matches led to their defeat.

Cultural Notes

When used to describe arguments or performances, 'feeble' implies that something is inadequate or weak compared to expectations. It is often used in critical contexts.

noun

Definition

a person or thing that is feeble in strength or quality

In ZH

在力量或质量上虚弱的人或事物

How to Use Feeble in a Sentence

  • He was considered a feeble in the group, unable to contribute effectively.
  • The feebles of the team were overshadowed by their more talented members.
  • In the society, the feebles were given special care and attention.

Cultural Notes

As a noun, 'feeble' can sometimes be used in antiquated or literary contexts to refer to those who are weak or vulnerable. It's less commonly used in modern vernacular.
