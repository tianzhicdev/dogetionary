Futilitarian

EN→ZH

What Does Futilitarian Mean?

  • 无用主义者
  • 徒劳者
  • 无效主义者

Futilitarian Pronunciation

/ˌfjuːtɪlɪˈtɛəriən/

Futilitarian Definition

noun

Definition

A person who believes in futility or that efforts are ultimately useless.

In ZH

一个相信无用论或认为努力最终无效的人。

How to Use Futilitarian in a Sentence

  • He is a futilitarian at heart, always doubting the purpose of his work.
  • Many writers and philosophers have been labeled as futilitarians due to their bleak outlook on life.
  • The futilitarian perspective often leads to a sense of despair among its adherents.
  • She argued that being a futilitarian is a self-fulfilling prophecy that hinders progress.
  • In discussions of existentialism, the term futilitarian often comes up as a critique of life's meaning.

Synonyms for Futilitarian

  • Futile
  • Futility
  • Futurism
