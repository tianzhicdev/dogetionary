Fetter

Pronunciation:/ˈfɛtər/
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 束缚
  • 限制
  • 脚镣
  • 桎梏

Definitions

noun

Definition

A chain or manacle used to restrain a prisoner, typically placed around the ankles.

In ZH

一种用于限制囚犯的链条或镣铐，通常放在脚踝上。

Examples

  • The prisoner was freed from his fetters.
  • She felt the fetters of society holding her back.
  • His past mistakes were like fetters on his present life.
  • The fetters of tradition can be difficult to break.
  • He was forced to wear fetters as a punishment.
verb

Definition

To restrain with chains or shackles.

In ZH

用链条或镣铐束缚。

Examples

  • They fettered his hands before the trial.
  • The regime fettered any dissenting voices.
  • Fear of failure can fetter one's ambitions.
  • She refused to let anyone fetter her creativity.
  • The law fetters individual freedom when it is too strict.
