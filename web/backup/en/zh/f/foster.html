Foster

Pronunciation:ˈfɔːstər
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 培养
  • 抚育
  • 促进
  • 照顾

Definitions

verb

Definition

To encourage or promote the development of something (typically a skill, relationship, or idea).

In ZH

促进或推动某事的发展（通常是技能、关系或想法）。

Examples

  • After watching his dog fetch the newspaper, Tim decided to foster his dog's new career as a delivery dog.
  • The school decided to foster creativity by letting students paint murals in the hallways – now they have a gallery that definitely needs a sign saying 'No Food Allowed!'
  • In an attempt to foster goodwill during the pandemic, the mayor suggested everyone wear mismatched socks as a symbol of unity – it surprisingly became a fashion trend!

Cultural Notes

Using 'foster' often implies nurturing or supporting something over time, rather than a one-time action. In modern contexts, it’s commonly used in discussions about education, community support, and family structures.

noun

Definition

The act of nurturing or caring for someone, typically used in the context of fostering children.

In ZH

抚养或照顾某人的行为，通常在照顾孩子的上下文中使用。

Examples

  • The charity focuses on providing foster homes for pets, because every puppy deserves to be spoiled!
  • She became a foster mother, claiming that she wanted to help kids, but her dog seemed to think she just wanted more toys in the house.
  • After becoming a foster parent, he remarked that his kids' ability to negotiate snack times was impressive – maybe they should run for office!

Cultural Notes

Fostering children often refers to temporary care, with an emphasis on love and support. It's a culturally significant practice in many countries, and there are often community programs that support foster families.

adjective

Definition

Relating to the care of someone else’s child, typically in a temporary arrangement.

In ZH

与照顾他人孩子有关，通常是临时照顾。

Examples

  • The foster family threw a welcome party for their new arrival, complete with balloons and a cake shaped like a dinosaur!
  • Foster kids sometimes have the best stories about their adventures, especially the ones where they convinced their foster parents to have ice cream for dinner!
  • When they decided to adopt their foster son, they threw a 'We are family!' concert in their backyard, despite not having any musical talent. If only the neighbors had known!

Cultural Notes

In many cultures, foster care is a crucial aspect of social services, often providing crucial stability for children in need. The adjective form 'foster' can also extend to relationships or initiatives that support growth.
