Fields

Pronunciation:fiːldz
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 田野
  • 领域
  • 场地

Definitions

noun

Definition

An area of open land, especially one planted with crops or pasture, typically bounded by hedges or fences.

In ZH

一块开放的土地，尤其是种植作物或牧草的，通常被树篱或篱笆围起来的地方。

Examples

  • The farmer planted corn in the fields this year.
  • We walked through the fields enjoying the fresh air.
  • The fields near my house are filled with wildflowers in spring.

Cultural Notes

In agriculture-focused cultures, fields are often seen as symbols of abundance and hard work.

noun

Definition

A particular branch of study or area of expertise.

In ZH

特定的研究领域或专业领域。

Examples

  • She has a degree in the field of biology.
  • Many innovations come from research in the field of technology.
  • He is well-known in the field of literature.

Cultural Notes

The idea of 'fields' in this context often relates to academic and professional life, where specialization is common.

verb

Definition

To cover an area with a certain type of crop or to manage a certain area of land for productive use.

In ZH

用特定类型的作物覆盖一个区域或管理某个地区以用于生产性用途。

Examples

  • They will field the new variety of wheat this season.
  • The organization is working to field more sustainable farming practices.
  • The government has programs to help farmers field their land effectively.

Cultural Notes

This usage is often found in discussions about agriculture and environmental management.
