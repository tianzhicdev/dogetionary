Faux Pas

EN→ZH

What Does Faux Pas Mean?

  • 失礼
  • 过失
  • 失态

Faux Pas Pronunciation

/foʊ pɑː/

Faux Pas Definition

noun

Definition

a socially awkward or tactless act; a blunder in etiquette

In ZH

一种社交上的尴尬或不得体的行为；礼节上的失误

How to Use Faux Pas in a Sentence

  • He committed a faux pas by arriving late to the formal dinner.
  • Wearing white to someone else's wedding is often considered a faux pas.
  • She pointed out his faux pas in front of the whole group, which embarrassed him.
  • The politician's faux pas during the interview led to widespread criticism.
  • Making an inappropriate joke in a serious meeting is a common faux pas.

Cultural Notes

In many cultures, faux pas can lead to social ostracism or damage to one's reputation.

Synonyms for Faux Pas

  • Faultless
  • Faun
