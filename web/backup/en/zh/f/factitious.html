Factitious

EN→ZH

What Does Factitious Mean?

  • 虚假的
  • 人为的
  • 假冒的

Factitious Pronunciation

/fækˈtɪʃəs/

Factitious Definition

adjective

Definition

artificially created or developed; not natural or genuine.

In ZH

人为创造或发展的；不自然或不真实的。

How to Use Factitious in a Sentence

  • The factitious symptoms were created by the patient for attention.
  • In the debate, he argued that their claims were factitious and lacked evidence.
  • Her enthusiasm seemed factitious, as if she was putting on a show.
  • The scientist noted that the factitious environment affected the results of the experiment.
  • After the scandal, the company faced backlash for its factitious advertising.

Synonyms for Factitious

  • Facetious
  • Facilitate
  • Facsimile
  • Factotum
