Flash

EN→ZH

What Does Flash Mean?

  • 闪光
  • 闪现
  • 速通
  • 突现

Flash Pronunciation

/flæʃ/

Flash Definition

noun

Definition

A sudden burst of light or a bright light that appears for a short time.

In ZH

一瞬间的光或短时间内出现的明亮光芒。

How to Use Flash in a Sentence

  • The flash of the camera startled me.
  • There was a flash of lightning in the sky.
  • He saw a flash of light out of the corner of his eye.
  • The flash from the fireworks lit up the night.
  • She turned on the flash to take a better photo.
verb

Definition

To produce a sudden burst of light or to shine briefly.

In ZH

产生一阵瞬间的光芒或短暂地发光。

How to Use Flash in a Sentence

  • The light flashed on and off.
  • He flashed his badge to get through the crowd.
  • The sign flashed brightly in the dark.
  • She flashed a smile before leaving the room.
  • The headlights of the car flashed as it drove by.
noun

Definition

A sudden occurrence or event that is surprising or striking.

In ZH

突然发生的让人惊讶或震惊的事件。

How to Use Flash in a Sentence

  • The news of her promotion was a flash in the media.
  • It was a flash of inspiration that led to his success.
  • The incident was just a flash of excitement in an otherwise dull day.
  • She experienced a flash of realization about her goals.
  • His rise to fame was a flash that no one expected.
noun

Definition

In technology, a term referring to memory storage devices that are fast and temporary.

In ZH

在技术中，指代速度快且临时的存储设备。

How to Use Flash in a Sentence

  • He saved the files on a flash drive.
  • The flash storage made data transfer much quicker.
  • She backed up her photos on a flash memory device.
  • Flash technology has revolutionized how we store data.
  • They used a flash disk to transfer files between computers.

Synonyms for Flash

  • Flabbergast
  • Flabbergasted
  • Flabbergastedness
  • Flabbergasting
  • Flagitious
