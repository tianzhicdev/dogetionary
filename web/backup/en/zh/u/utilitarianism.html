Utilitarianism

EN→ZH

What Does Utilitarianism Mean?

  • 功利主义

Utilitarianism Pronunciation

/juːˌtɪlɪˈtɛəriənɪzəm/

Utilitarianism Definition

noun

Definition

A philosophical theory that suggests that the best action is the one that maximizes utility, usually defined as that which produces the greatest well-being of the greatest number of people.

In ZH

一种哲学理论，建议最佳行动是最大化效用的行动，通常定义为能为最大数量的人产生最大幸福感的行为。

How to Use Utilitarianism in a Sentence

  • Utilitarianism encourages actions that promote the greatest happiness for the greatest number.
  • Many political philosophies are influenced by utilitarianism.
  • In a utilitarian framework, the best decision is the one that results in the most positive outcomes.
  • Critics of utilitarianism argue that it can justify harmful actions if they benefit the majority.
  • Utilitarianism is often discussed in the context of ethical decision-making.

Synonyms for Utilitarianism
