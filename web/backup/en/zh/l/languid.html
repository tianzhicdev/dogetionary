Languid

EN→ZH

What Does Languid Mean?

  • 无力的
  • 疲倦的
  • 萎靡的

Languid Pronunciation

/ˈlæŋɡwɪd/

Languid Definition

adjective

Definition

Weak or faint from illness or fatigue; lacking energy or vitality.

In ZH

因疾病或疲劳而虚弱或微弱；缺乏能量或活力。

How to Use Languid in a Sentence

  • After the long hike, I felt languid and needed to rest.
  • The heat of the afternoon made everyone feel languid and sleepy.
  • She spoke in a languid voice, barely able to keep her eyes open.
  • The languid waves lapped gently against the shore.
  • He had a languid style of painting that reflected his relaxed personality.

Synonyms for Languid

  • Languish
  • Languor
  • Languorous
  • Lantana
