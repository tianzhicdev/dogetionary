Lash Out

EN→ZH

What Does Lash Out Mean?

  • 发作
  • 攻击
  • 猛烈反击

Lash Out Pronunciation

læʃ aʊt

Lash Out Definition

verb

Definition

To suddenly and angrily criticize or attack someone verbally or physically.

In ZH

突然且愤怒地口头或身体攻击或批评某人。

How to Use Lash Out in a Sentence

  • When he was provoked, he tended to lash out at his friends.
  • After losing the game, she lashed out at her teammates.
  • It's not appropriate to lash out when you're feeling angry; try to express your feelings calmly.

Cultural Notes

In many cultures, expressing anger is often viewed as negative; however, 'lashing out' sometimes highlights the importance of addressing feelings. It's commonly seen in stressful situations, where individuals might say something hurtful without considering the consequences.

noun

Definition

An instance of suddenly and angrily attacking someone verbally or physically.

In ZH

突然且愤怒地攻击某人（口头或身体上的）事件。

How to Use Lash Out in a Sentence

  • His lash out during the meeting shocked everyone.
  • She had a brief lash out at her boss after receiving criticism.
  • The lash out from the audience surprised the speaker.

Cultural Notes

This noun form highlights specific moments of outburst. It's important to understand that while 'lashing out' can be seen as a loss of control, it can also signify a deeper emotional struggle. In many cultures, managing emotions in a public setting is highly valued.

Synonyms for Lash Out

  • Lascivious
  • Lash
  • Lassitude
