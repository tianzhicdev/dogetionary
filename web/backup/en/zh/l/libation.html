Libation

EN→ZH

What Does Libation Mean?

  • 祭品
  • 饮品
  • 干杯

Libation Pronunciation

/laɪˈbeɪʃən/

Libation Definition

noun

Definition

A ritual pouring of a liquid as an offering to a deity or spirit.

In ZH

作为对神或灵的祭品而倾倒液体的仪式。

How to Use Libation in a Sentence

  • In ancient Greece, a libation was often poured to honor the gods.
  • During the ceremony, they made a libation to seek blessings for the harvest.
  • He offered a libation of wine to the spirits of his ancestors.
  • The priest performed a libation, pouring oil on the altar.
  • Libations were a common practice in many indigenous cultures.

Cultural Notes

In many cultures, libations are an important part of rituals and ceremonies, often symbolizing respect and reverence for deities.

Synonyms for Libation

  • Libidinous
  • Libretto
