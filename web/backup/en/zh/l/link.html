Link

EN→ZH

What Does Link Mean?

  • 链接
  • 联系
  • 连接

Link Pronunciation

lɪŋk

Link Definition

noun

Definition

A relationship or connection between people, things, or ideas.

In ZH

人与人、事物或观念之间的关系或联系。

How to Use Link in a Sentence

  • The only link between the two cats is their shared love for tuna. You could say they are 'fin'-tastic friends!
  • After the scandal, the only link to the politician's reputation was a broken email chain!
  • Scientists found a surprising link between with too much screen time and cats taking over the internet!

Cultural Notes

In modern contexts, 'link' often refers to online connections, like hyperlinks on websites.

verb

Definition

To connect or join two or more things together.

In ZH

把两个或多个事物连接或结合在一起。

How to Use Link in a Sentence

  • I tried to link my yoga routine with my donut-eating habit, but it didn't quite work out!
  • Every time I link my email account to my phone, I receive more spam than a canning factory!
  • She linked her hair dryer to a power outlet and accidentally transformed her home into a tornado zone!

Cultural Notes

Linking ideas together is common in writing and communication. In tech, it refers to connecting software or devices.

noun

Definition

A physical or virtual connection, such as a hyperlink on the internet.

In ZH

一种物理或虚拟的连接，如互联网上的超链接。

How to Use Link in a Sentence

  • I clicked on the link to the news article, and suddenly my day turned into a three-hour rabbit hole!
  • Google became my best friend when I followed the link to a recipe that promised to make dinner in 5 minutes – and it only took an hour!
  • He got lost in cyberspace after following a link that led him to a 'cat dressed as a taco' website!

Cultural Notes

In the digital age, understanding links is crucial for navigating the internet and finding information.
