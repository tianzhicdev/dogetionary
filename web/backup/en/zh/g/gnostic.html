Gnostic

EN→ZH

What Does Gnostic Mean?

  • 诺斯替教的
  • 诺斯底主义者

Gnostic Pronunciation

/ˈnɒstɪk/

Gnostic Definition

noun

Definition

a member of a religious movement in the early Christian era that emphasized personal spiritual knowledge above orthodox teachings and institutions.

In ZH

一个生活在早期基督教时代的宗教运动的成员，该运动强调个人灵性知识高于正统教义和机构。

How to Use Gnostic in a Sentence

  • Early gnostics believed that salvation came through knowledge.
  • The gnostic texts were discovered in the Nag Hammadi library.
  • Many gnostic sects had their own interpretations of creation.
  • Gnostics often viewed the material world as flawed or evil.
  • The gnostic tradition influenced various branches of Christianity.

Cultural Notes

Gnosticism incorporates elements of Christianity, Judaism, and Hellenistic philosophy, and presents a dualistic view of the world.

adjective

Definition

relating to the Gnostics or their beliefs.

In ZH

与诺斯替教徒或他们的信仰相关的。

How to Use Gnostic in a Sentence

  • The gnostic texts offer alternative views on the creation story.
  • Some scholars study gnostic philosophies in relation to modern spirituality.
  • Gnostic interpretations of God differ significantly from orthodox views.
  • He was fascinated by the gnostic elements in early Christian writings.
  • Their gnostic beliefs challenged the authority of the church.

Cultural Notes

Gnostic beliefs often challenge traditional views of God and creation, emphasizing inner knowledge and enlightenment.

Synonyms for Gnostic

  • Gnomic
  • Gnoshing
  • Gnosis
  • Gnosticism
