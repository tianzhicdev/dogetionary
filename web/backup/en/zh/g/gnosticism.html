Gnosticism

EN→ZH

What Does Gnosticism Mean?

  • 诺斯替主义
  • 灵知主义

Gnosticism Pronunciation

/ˈnɒstɪsɪz(ə)m/

Gnosticism Definition

noun

Definition

A religious movement in the early Christian era that emphasized personal spiritual knowledge (gnosis) over orthodox teachings and institutions.

In ZH

一种早期基督教时代的宗教运动，强调个人的精神知识（认知）重于正统教义和机构。

How to Use Gnosticism in a Sentence

  • Gnosticism was influential among early Christians.
  • Many Gnostic texts were discovered at Nag Hammadi.
  • The Gnostic worldview often contrasts sharply with orthodox Christianity.
  • Gnosticism seeks knowledge of the divine through inner experience.
  • Some consider Gnosticism as a heretical offshoot of early Christianity.

Cultural Notes

Gnosticism is often studied in the context of early Christian history and its divergence from mainstream Christian beliefs.

Synonyms for Gnosticism

  • Gnomic
  • Gnoshing
  • Gnosis
  • Gnostic
