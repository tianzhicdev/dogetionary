Glut

EN→ZH

What Does Glut Mean?

  • 过剩
  • 大量
  • 盈余

Glut Pronunciation

/ɡlʌt/

Glut Definition

noun

Definition

an excessive supply or abundance of something

In ZH

过量供应或丰富的东西

How to Use Glut in a Sentence

  • There is a glut of unsold cars on the market.
  • The region experienced a glut of agricultural products this year.
  • The company faced a glut of competition in the tech industry.
  • After the holidays, there is often a glut of returned items in stores.
  • The film festival showcased a glut of new indie films.
verb

Definition

to fill beyond capacity; to flood with something in abundance

In ZH

过度填充；用大量东西泛滥

How to Use Glut in a Sentence

  • They decided to glut the market with new smartphones.
  • She glutted herself on sweets at the party.
  • The warehouse was glutted with excess inventory.
  • During the harvest, farmers sometimes glut the local markets.
  • The artist glutted her gallery with too many paintings.

Synonyms for Glut

  • Glutton
