Garrison

EN→ZH

What Does Garrison Mean?

  • 驻军
  • 守卫部队
  • 兵营

Garrison Pronunciation

/ˈɡer.ɪ.sən/

Garrison Definition

noun

Definition

A military post or the troops stationed at a specific location.

In ZH

军事驻地或驻守在特定地点的部队。

How to Use Garrison in a Sentence

  • The garrison was well fortified against enemy attacks.
  • They stationed a garrison in the town to maintain order.
  • The soldiers returned to their garrison after the mission.
  • A large garrison was established to protect the region.
  • The garrison troops are trained for quick deployment.
verb

Definition

To provide troops to occupy a place, especially to defend it.

In ZH

提供部队以占领一个地方，特别是为了保护它。

How to Use Garrison in a Sentence

  • The army garrisoned forces along the border.
  • They garrisoned the castle to prevent invasions.
  • The general decided to garrison the city after the attack.
  • To crush the rebellion, the government garrisoned several regiments there.
  • The military garrisoned personnel at the strategic location.

Synonyms for Garrison

  • Garamond
  • Gargantuan
  • Gargoyle
  • Garish
  • Garnet
