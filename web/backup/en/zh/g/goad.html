Goad

EN→ZH

What Does Goad Mean?

  • 刺激
  • 驱使
  • 激励

Goad Pronunciation

/ɡoʊd/

Goad Definition

verb

Definition

To provoke or annoy someone so as to stimulate some action or reaction.

In ZH

激怒某人以刺激其行动或反应。

How to Use Goad in a Sentence

  • She goaded him into applying for the job.
  • He was goaded by his friends to join the competition.
  • The teacher goaded her students to improve their grades.
  • They goaded the team to play harder during the match.
  • I tried to goad him into talking about his feelings.
noun

Definition

A stick with a pointed or electrically charged end, for driving cattle or other livestock.

In ZH

一种有尖端或带电的棍子，用于驱赶牛或其他牲畜。

How to Use Goad in a Sentence

  • He used a goad to lead the cows to the pasture.
  • The farmer carried a goad to control the herd.
  • A goad is essential for moving stubborn animals.
  • They employed a goad to keep the sheep in line.
  • Using a goad, the shepherd directed the flock.

Synonyms for Goad

  • Goal
