Get Around

Pronunciation:ɡɛt əˈraʊnd
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 到处走动
  • 周旋
  • （消息等）传播

Definitions

verb

Definition

To move from one place to another, especially when it involves travel or navigating within a city or area.

In ZH

在一个地方到另一个地方移动，尤其是当涉及旅行或在城市或地区内导航时。

Examples

  • I like to get around the city on my bicycle.
  • It's easy to get around the downtown area on foot.
  • She often gets around by using public transportation.

Cultural Notes

In many cities, getting around efficiently can be crucial to experiencing the local culture and attractions.

verb

Definition

To manage to deal with something or someone, especially in a clever or resourceful manner.

In ZH

设法处理某事或某人，尤其是以聪明或足智多谋的方式。

Examples

  • He knows how to get around difficult situations.
  • She can get around the rules to make things work to her advantage.
  • You have to get around the local customs when traveling abroad.

Cultural Notes

This usage highlights the importance of social skills and adaptability in different situations, often reflecting cultural norms.

idiomatic expression

Definition

To become known or spread, especially news or information.

In ZH

变得为人所知或传播，尤其是新闻或信息。

Examples

  • The news of the event got around quickly.
  • Once the rumor started, it didn't take long for it to get around.
  • I heard about the party; it got around faster than I expected.

Cultural Notes

This reflects how quickly information can spread in today's digital age, especially through social media.
