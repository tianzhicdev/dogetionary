Embellishing

Pronunciation:ɪmˈbɛl.ɪʃ.ɪŋ
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 修饰
  • 渲染
  • 美化

Definitions

verb

Definition

To make (something) more attractive by the addition of decorative details or features.

In ZH

通过增加装饰细节或特征使（某物）更具吸引力。

Examples

  • She spent hours embellishing the wedding invitations with ribbons and lace.
  • The artist is known for embellishing her paintings with intricate details.
  • He embellished the story to make it more interesting.

Cultural Notes

In many cultures, embellishing is common in storytelling as a way to engage the audience and make the narrative more appealing. However, it's important to balance embellishments with sincerity, especially in personal anecdotes.

adjective

Definition

Describing something that has been decorated or enhanced in a way that adds beauty or interest.

In ZH

描述某事物以美丽或趣味的方式被装饰或增强。

Examples

  • The embellished dress sparkled in the sunlight.
  • He admired the embellished manuscripts from the medieval period.
  • Her embellished speech captivated everyone at the event.

Cultural Notes

Embellished items, such as clothing or decor, are often seen as more luxurious and are culturally significant in celebrating special occasions.
