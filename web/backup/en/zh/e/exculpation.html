Exculpation

Pronunciation:/ˌɛks.kʌlˈpeɪ.ʃən/
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 免责
  • 辩解
  • 洗脱罪名

Definitions

noun

Definition

The act of freeing someone from blame or fault; a justification or explanation that absolves someone from guilt.

In ZH

使某人免除责任或过失的行为；可以使某人免受罪责的辩解或解释。

Examples

  • The defendant's exculpation was based on the new evidence presented.
  • Her letter served as an exculpation in the court hearings.
  • He sought exculpation for his actions after the incident.
  • The exculpation process took longer than expected due to the complexity of the case.
  • In light of recent developments, the exculpation was well-received by the public.
