Enduring

EN→ZH

What Does Enduring Mean?

  • 持久的
  • 耐久的
  • 永恒的

Enduring Pronunciation

ɪnˈdʊrɪŋ

Enduring Definition

adjective

Definition

Lasting over a period of time; able to withstand wear or decay.

In ZH

持续一段时间的；能够耐受磨损或衰退的。

How to Use Enduring in a Sentence

  • Her enduring love for him stayed strong through the years.
  • The enduring quality of this fabric makes it ideal for outdoor use.
  • They formed an enduring friendship that lasted a lifetime.

Cultural Notes

In English, 'enduring' often connotes a sense of resilience and strength. It can apply to relationships, materials, and even ideas that have proven to stand the test of time.

verb

Definition

To undergo (something painful or challenging) without giving way; to tolerate.

In ZH

经历（痛苦或挑战）而不屈服；忍受。

How to Use Enduring in a Sentence

  • She endured the pain of the injury with great courage.
  • Many people endure hardships in life, yet they emerge stronger.
  • He had to endure a long wait at the airport.

Cultural Notes

The verb 'endure' emphasizes the struggle involved in facing difficulties. It is often used in storytelling and narratives where characters face challenges.

Synonyms for Enduring

  • Endeavor
  • Endings
  • Endorsed
  • Endure
