Epithet

EN→ZH

What Does Epithet Mean?

  • 称谓
  • 修饰语
  • 表述词

Epithet Pronunciation

/ˈɛpɪθɛt/

Epithet Definition

noun

Definition

A descriptive word or phrase that is used to characterize someone or something.

In ZH

用于表征某人或某事的描述性词语或短语。

How to Use Epithet in a Sentence

  • The poet used the epithet 'swift-footed' to describe the hero.
  • In his speech, he referred to her as 'the queen of compassion', an epithet that resonated with the audience.
  • The term 'the great' is often used as an epithet for historical figures like Alexander.
  • Many writers use epithets to create vivid imagery in their works.
  • In ancient times, epithets were commonly used in literature and oral traditions.

Cultural Notes

In literature, especially in epic poetry, epithets serve to add rhythm and enhance character recognition, making them a common stylistic device.

Synonyms for Epithet

  • Epicene
  • Epicurean
  • Epicureanism
  • Epigraph
  • Epiphanic
