Endorsed

Pronunciation:ɪnˈdɔrst
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 支持
  • 背书
  • 认可

Definitions

verb

Definition

To declare one's public approval or support of something or someone.

In ZH

公开宣布对某事或某人的支持或认可。

Examples

  • The cat endorsed the new catnip as the best thing since sliced bread, after experiencing a 12-hour nap.
  • In a shocking twist, the mayor endorsed a pizza place as a legitimate tax write-off after 'working' there for hours.
  • The latest superhero movie was endorsed by a dog influencer, who claimed it was 'pawsitively amazing!'

Cultural Notes

In American politics, endorsements from celebrities or other influential figures can significantly impact public opinion and voting behavior.

adjective

Definition

Having received approval or support from someone influential or authoritative.

In ZH

获得来自有影响力或权威人士的认可或支持。

Examples

  • The endorsed product was so popular that it started to sell like hotcakes, but the chicken was still mad it couldn't get a deal too.
  • After being endorsed by the local taco truck, the dessert shop suddenly had customers lining up to try their chili chocolate cupcakes.
  • He felt like a rockstar after his baking skills were endorsed by the town's chef, who never shared secrets.

Cultural Notes

Products often use endorsements from famous personalities to gain credibility and attract customers.
