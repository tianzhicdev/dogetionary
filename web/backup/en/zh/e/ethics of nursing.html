Ethics Of Nursing

Pronunciation:ˈɛθɪks əv ˈnɜːrsɪŋ
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 护理伦理
  • 护理道德

Definitions

noun

Definition

The principles and standards that govern the conduct of nurses in their professional practice, emphasizing the importance of patient care, confidentiality, and moral responsibility.

In ZH

护士在其专业实践中规范行为的原则和标准，强调患者护理、保密和道德责任的重要性。

Examples

  • After rescuing a cat from a tree, Nurse Sarah joked, 'We should have a new section in our ethics of nursing handbook: 'Climbing Skills for Fur-Bearing Patients!'
  • During a conference, a nurse quipped, 'The ethics of nursing? You mean the art of juggling coffee, compassion, and crazy patients without spilling!'
  • Nurse Tom pondered, 'If only our ethics of nursing included a clause for dealing with patients who think they’re part of a reality show!'

Cultural Notes

In nursing, ethics often intersect with cultural expectations and norms, illustrating the importance of sensitivity to patients' diverse backgrounds and values.

plural noun

Definition

A set of moral principles that guide the behavior of nurses as they make decisions in their practice, including aspects like advocacy, responsibility, and justice.

In ZH

指导护士在实践中做决策的道德原则，包括倡导、责任和公正等方面。

Examples

  • While debating ethics of nursing, Nurse Lisa half-joked, 'So, does maintaining patient dignity include refusing to let them wear those awful hospital gowns?'
  • In a discussion about correct procedures, a nurse laughed, 'The ethics of nursing should really include a morale boost—like giving out superhero capes for night shifts!'
  • When discussing ethics of nursing, one nurse quipped, 'If only our patients came with an instruction manual, we could skip all this ethical debating!'

Cultural Notes

Understanding ethics in nursing not only enhances care but also fosters trust, especially important in cultures that highly regard familial and communal relationships.
