Effete

EN→ZH

What Does Effete Mean?

  • 衰弱的
  • 无用的
  • 缺乏活力的

Effete Pronunciation

/ɪˈfiːt/

Effete Definition

adjective

Definition

Lacking in wholesome vigor; degenerate; effeminate.

In ZH

缺乏健康活力；退化的；女性化的。

How to Use Effete in a Sentence

  • The once powerful empire had grown effete and complacent.
  • His effete mannerisms were often mocked by his peers.
  • She found the effete lifestyle of the city unappealing.
  • Many considered the artist's style to be too effete.
  • In a world that demands strength, being effete can be seen as a weakness.

Cultural Notes

The term 'effete' often carries a negative connotation, suggesting not only weakness but also a loss of vitality typical of a once vigorous society.
