Enter

EN→ZH

What Does Enter Mean?

  • 进入
  • 登记
  • 参加

Enter Pronunciation

ˈɛn.tər

Enter Definition

verb

Definition

To come or go into a place.

In ZH

进入一个地方。

How to Use Enter in a Sentence

  • Please enter the room quietly.
  • She entered the competition and won first place.
  • He entered the data into the computer.

Cultural Notes

In English-speaking cultures, it is common to say 'enter' when someone walks into a room or building, implying a transition from outside to inside.

verb

Definition

To register or make a record of something, such as information or a name.

In ZH

记录或登记某事，例如信息或姓名。

How to Use Enter in a Sentence

  • You need to enter your name on the list.
  • Make sure to enter your details correctly on the form.
  • He entered the information into the database.

Cultural Notes

This usage of 'enter' is frequently encountered in administrative or bureaucratic contexts, such as filling out forms or quality assurance procedures.

verb

Definition

To take part in an event or competition.

In ZH

参加一个事件或比赛。

How to Use Enter in a Sentence

  • They plan to enter the marathon next year.
  • You can enter the contest by submitting your artwork.
  • She decided to enter two categories in the talent show.

Cultural Notes

Entering competitions is common in various aspects of life, from sports to arts, and is often seen as a way of challenging oneself.

Synonyms for Enter
