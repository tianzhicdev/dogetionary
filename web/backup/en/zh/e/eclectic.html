Eclectic

EN→ZH

What Does Eclectic Mean?

  • 折衷的
  • 兼收并蓄的
  • 博采众长的

Eclectic Pronunciation

/ɪˈklɛktɪk/

Eclectic Definition

adjective

Definition

Deriving ideas, style, or taste from a broad and diverse range of sources.

In ZH

从广泛而多样的来源获取思想、风格或品味。

How to Use Eclectic in a Sentence

  • Her eclectic taste in music includes everything from classical to hip hop.
  • The restaurant's menu is eclectic, featuring dishes from various international cuisines.
  • He has an eclectic collection of art from different periods and styles.
  • Their approach to education is eclectic, incorporating various teaching methods.
  • The interior design of the house is eclectic, blending modern and vintage elements.

Cultural Notes

Eclecticism is often valued in art and philosophy for bringing together diverse influences.
