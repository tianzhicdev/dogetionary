Patting

EN→ZH

What Does Patting Mean?

  • 轻拍
  • 拍打
  • 抚摸

Patting Pronunciation

ˈpæt.ɪŋ

Patting Definition

noun

Definition

The action of lightly touching someone or something with the palm of the hand.

In ZH

用手掌轻轻触碰某人或某物的动作。

How to Use Patting in a Sentence

  • After losing the match, the coach gave the players a pat on the back, but I think he forgot it was a virtual game this time!
  • I tried patting my cat to comfort her, but she looked at me like I just announced my candidacy for President!
  • The dog was so excited that every time I stopped patting him, he looked at me like I’d just canceled his favorite TV show.

Cultural Notes

In many cultures, patting someone on the back can be seen as a gesture of encouragement or support. However, its appropriateness can vary depending on the context and relationship between individuals.

verb

Definition

To touch someone or something lightly, often repeatedly, with your hand.

In ZH

用手轻轻、通常是重复地触碰某人或某物。

How to Use Patting in a Sentence

  • I was patting my pockets for my phone, and my friend thought I was practicing for a game of invisible tag!
  • During the interview, I was patting down my nervousness, but it turns out my shirt was just as wrinkled as my plans for the weekend!
  • He is always patting the dog, but the dog just thinks he's auditioning for a reality show called 'Who Can Pet the Best!'

Cultural Notes

The act of patting can convey a range of emotions, from love and affection to reassurance and comfort. It is commonly used in domestic settings with pets and children.

Synonyms for Patting

  • Pathological
  • Pathos
  • Patina
