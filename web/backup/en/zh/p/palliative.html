Palliative

Pronunciation:/ˈpæl.ɪ.ə.tɪv/
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 缓和的
  • 姑息的
  • 舒缓的

Definitions

adjective

Definition

Relieving pain or alleviating a problem without dealing with the underlying cause.

In ZH

缓解疼痛或减轻问题，而不处理根本原因。

Examples

  • Palliative care focuses on providing relief to patients with serious illnesses.
  • The doctor recommended palliative treatments to improve the patient's quality of life.
  • Hospitals increasingly offer palliative services alongside traditional medical treatments.
  • The aim of palliative medicine is to help patients feel comfortable and supported.
  • Her palliative approach made a significant difference in managing his pain.
noun

Definition

A drug or treatment that eases symptoms but does not cure the disease.

In ZH

减轻症状但不治愈疾病的药物或治疗方法。

Examples

  • Many palliative medications are available for managing chronic pain.
  • The nurse administered a palliative to help with the patient's discomfort.
  • Palliative care teams often include doctors, nurses, and social workers.
  • The use of palliatives in terminal illnesses can improve the patient's last days.
  • In many cultures, palliative care is seen as an essential part of end-of-life planning.
