Patina

EN→ZH

What Does Patina Mean?

  • 包浆
  • 铜绿
  • 光泽
  • 表面

Patina Pronunciation

/pəˈtiːnə/

Patina Definition

noun

Definition

A green or brown film on the surface of bronze or similar metals, produced by oxidation over a long period.

In ZH

在青铜或类似金属表面上由氧化长时间产生的绿色或棕色薄膜。

How to Use Patina in a Sentence

  • The statue had a beautiful patina after years of exposure to the elements.
  • The artist favored a technique that would enhance the patina of the metal.
  • The old door had developed a rich patina that told its history.
  • Restorers carefully preserved the patina on the ancient artifact.
  • The patina of the copper roof added character to the historic building.
noun

Definition

A superficial layer that exhibits change over time, especially in the context of furniture or art.

In ZH

在时间的推移中表现出变化的表面层，尤其在家具或艺术的背景下。

How to Use Patina in a Sentence

  • The antique table had a lovely patina that made it feel warm and inviting.
  • A well-maintained patina can increase the value of a vintage piece.
  • The patina on the painting illustrated its age and authenticity.
  • Collectors seek items with a natural patina for their charm.
  • The patina of the wooden sculpture highlighted its craftsmanship.

Synonyms for Patina

  • Pathological
  • Pathos
  • Patting
