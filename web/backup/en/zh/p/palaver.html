Palaver

Pronunciation:/pəˈlæ.vər/
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 空谈
  • 讨论
  • 闲聊

Definitions

noun

Definition

A prolonged and idle discussion; excessive talk or chatter.

In ZH

漫长而无目的的讨论；过度的谈话或闲聊。

Examples

  • They engaged in palaver about the best places to eat in the city.
  • The meeting turned into a palaver that lasted hours without reaching a decision.
  • His constant palaver about politics bored everyone at the dinner table.
  • We had a palaver about our summer plans before deciding on a trip.
  • Her palaver was entertaining, but it strayed far from the main topic.

Cultural Notes

In some cultures, 'palaver' can refer to a formal discussion or debate, often with an undertone of frivolity.

verb

Definition

To talk unnecessarily or at length; to engage in palaver.

In ZH

不必要或冗长地谈论；进行闲聊。

Examples

  • They palavered for hours without getting to the point.
  • She tends to palaver when she's nervous, filling the silence with chatter.
  • He palavered on about his trip, not realizing nobody was listening.
  • We could have finished the task quicker if we hadn't palavered so much.
  • Let's not palaver; let's get to the heart of the matter.

Cultural Notes

Using 'palaver' as a verb often implies that the talk is considered pointless or excessive.
