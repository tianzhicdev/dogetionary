Panopticon

EN→ZH

What Does Panopticon Mean?

  • 全景监狱
  • 监视器
  • 全景监视

Panopticon Pronunciation

/pæˈnɒptɪkən/

Panopticon Definition

noun

Definition

A circular prison with cells arranged around a central well, from which prisoners could at all times be observed without knowing whether they are being watched.

In ZH

一种圆形监狱，囚室围绕着一个中央井，囚犯可以随时被观察，而不知道自己是否正在被监视。

How to Use Panopticon in a Sentence

  • The concept of the panopticon was proposed by Jeremy Bentham.
  • In a panopticon, the design allows for constant observation of inmates.
  • The panopticon serves as a metaphor for modern surveillance society.
  • Many modern buildings are inspired by the original panopticon design.
  • The idea of the panopticon raises questions about privacy and control.

Synonyms for Panopticon

  • Panegyric
  • Panoply
  • Panopticism
