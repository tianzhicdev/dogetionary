Pantheon

EN→ZH

What Does Pantheon Mean?

  • 万神殿
  • 众神
  • 神祇

Pantheon Pronunciation

/ˈpænθiən/

Pantheon Definition

noun

Definition

A group of particularly respected, famous, or important people.

In ZH

特别受尊敬、著名或重要的一群人。

How to Use Pantheon in a Sentence

  • The pantheon of great writers includes Shakespeare and Tolstoy.
  • In the pantheon of sports heroes, Michael Jordan stands out.
  • Scientists are often recognized in the pantheon of human achievement.
  • The museum celebrated the pantheon of artists in the 20th century.
  • In many cultures, the pantheon of gods reflects societal values.
noun

Definition

All the gods of a particular religion or mythology.

In ZH

特定宗教或神话中的所有神。

How to Use Pantheon in a Sentence

  • The Greek pantheon is well-known for its fascinating stories.
  • In Hinduism, the pantheon includes a vast array of deities.
  • The ancient Romans worshipped a pantheon that mirrored the Greek gods.
  • The pantheon of Chinese mythology features numerous legendary figures.
  • Scholars study the pantheon of gods to understand ancient cultures.

Synonyms for Pantheon

  • Panacea
  • Pandemonium
  • Panegyric
  • Panoply
  • Panopticism
