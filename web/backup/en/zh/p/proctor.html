Proctor

EN→ZH

What Does Proctor Mean?

  • 监考者
  • 代理人
  • 监督者

Proctor Pronunciation

/ˈprɒktə/

Proctor Definition

noun

Definition

A person who supervises students during an examination.

In ZH

在考试期间监督学生的人。

How to Use Proctor in a Sentence

  • The proctor walked around the room to ensure no one was cheating.
  • Students must show their IDs to the proctor before entering the exam room.
  • A proctor was appointed to oversee the final exam process.
  • The exam will be conducted under the supervision of a proctor.
  • She was nervous about having a proctor watch her take the test.
verb

Definition

To supervise or oversee an examination or test.

In ZH

监督或管理考试或测试。

How to Use Proctor in a Sentence

  • She was asked to proctor the standardized tests next week.
  • He needed someone to proctor the exam while he was away.
  • The school decided to proctor the final exams online this year.
  • It’s important to proctor exams to maintain academic integrity.
  • Can you proctor the assessment if I am unavailable?

Synonyms for Proctor

  • Probity
  • Process
  • Proclivity
  • Procrastinate
  • Procrastination
