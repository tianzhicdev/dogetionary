Period

Pronunciation:/ˈpɪəriəd/
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 期
  • 周期
  • 时期
  • 点
  • 句点

Definitions

noun

Definition

A length or portion of time.

In ZH

一段或一部分时间。

Examples

  • The project will take a period of six months to complete.
  • We can only hold the event for a short period.
  • During this period, many changes took place in the organization.
  • She went through a difficult period in her life.
  • The period of the Renaissance was marked by great cultural advancements.
noun

Definition

A punctuation mark (.) used at the end of a sentence.

In ZH

一个标点符号（.），用于句子的末尾。

Examples

  • Make sure to end your sentences with a period.
  • She forgot to put a period at the end of her sentence.
  • In English, a period indicates the end of a statement.
  • The teacher pointed out that there was no period at the end.
  • A period is essential for clarity in writing.
noun

Definition

The time during which a particular event or condition exists.

In ZH

特定事件或状态存在的时间。

Examples

  • This period of history is fascinating to study.
  • The festival lasts for a period of two weeks.
  • We've entered a new period of economic growth.
  • They lived in a period of great uncertainty.
  • The period before the exam is always stressful.
