Permeate

EN→ZH

What Does Permeate Mean?

  • 渗透
  • 弥漫
  • 遍及

Permeate Pronunciation

ˈpɜːr.mi.eɪt

Permeate Definition

verb

Definition

to spread through or penetrate something; to pass into or through something.

In ZH

通过或渗透某物；进入或穿过某物。

How to Use Permeate in a Sentence

  • The smell of fresh bread began to permeate the entire house.
  • Ideas of freedom and equality permeate our culture.
  • Water can permeate the soil, helping plants to grow.

Cultural Notes

In many cultures, the concept of permeation can be applied to both physical and abstract ideas, like how emotions or culture can permeate social settings.

verb

Definition

to become diffused throughout every part of; to saturate.

In ZH

在每一部分中扩散；使饱和。

How to Use Permeate in a Sentence

  • The influence of technology permeates modern education.
  • Fear began to permeate the community after the announcement.
  • Her laughter seemed to permeate the room, making everyone smile.

Cultural Notes

In literary contexts, 'permeate' is often used to describe how themes or motifs spread through a narrative.

Synonyms for Permeate

  • Perambulate
  • Perceived
  • Perchance
  • Percolate
  • Perdition
