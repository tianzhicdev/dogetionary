Panopticism

Pronunciation:/pænˈɒptɪsɪzəm/
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 全景监视主义
  • 全景监控论
  • 全视监控理论

Definitions

noun

Definition

A social theory that argues for the surveillance of the populace and the power dynamics involved in surveillance practices.

In ZH

一种社会理论，主张对公众进行监控及监控实践中涉及的权力动态。

Examples

  • The concept of panopticism is often discussed in the context of modern surveillance technologies.
  • In a panoptic society, individuals may alter their behavior due to the awareness of being watched.
  • Foucault's discussion of panopticism highlights the relationship between power and knowledge.
  • Many argue that panopticism has become a defining characteristic of the digital age.
  • Critics of panopticism suggest that constant surveillance can lead to a loss of privacy.
