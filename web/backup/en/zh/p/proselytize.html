Proselytize

EN→ZH

What Does Proselytize Mean?

  • 劝诱
  • 改宗
  • 传播信仰

Proselytize Pronunciation

/ˈprɒs.ə.lɪ.taɪz/

Proselytize Definition

verb

Definition

To convert or attempt to convert someone from one religion, belief, or opinion to another.

In ZH

试图将某人从一种宗教、信仰或观点转变为另一种宗教、信仰或观点。

How to Use Proselytize in a Sentence

  • Many missionaries travel to different countries to proselytize their faith.
  • She felt it was her duty to proselytize and share her beliefs with others.
  • He was known for his efforts to proselytize among young adults.
  • The organization aims to proselytize its values through community outreach.
  • Historically, various religions have sought to proselytize new followers.

Synonyms for Proselytize

  • Probity
  • Process
  • Proclivity
  • Procrastinate
  • Procrastination
