Prescience

EN→ZH

What Does Prescience Mean?

  • 先见之明
  • 预知
  • 预见

Prescience Pronunciation

/ˈprɛsɪəns/

Prescience Definition

noun

Definition

The ability to know or predict what will happen in the future.

In ZH

预知未来的能力。

How to Use Prescience in a Sentence

  • Her prescience in business decisions led to significant profits.
  • Many scientists claim that a prescience about climate change is evident in their warnings.
  • His prescience allowed him to avoid the pitfalls that others fell into.
  • The prescience of certain historical figures has proven to be remarkably accurate over time.
  • Some believe that intuition is a form of prescience.

Synonyms for Prescience

  • Precarious
  • Precariousness
  • Precipitate
  • Precocious
  • Predilection
