Propitiate

EN→ZH

What Does Propitiate Mean?

  • 安抚
  • 取悦
  • 平息

Propitiate Pronunciation

/prəˈpɪʃ.i.eɪt/

Propitiate Definition

verb

Definition

to win or regain the favor or goodwill of someone, especially by doing something that pleases them

In ZH

赢得或恢复某人的青睐或好感，特别是通过做一些让他们高兴的事情

How to Use Propitiate in a Sentence

  • To propitiate the angry gods, the villagers offered sacrifices.
  • She tried to propitiate her parents after arriving home late.
  • He sought to propitiate his boss with a sincere apology.
  • The diplomat worked hard to propitiate the foreign leader's concerns.
  • In ancient cultures, people would propitiate their deities to ensure a good harvest.

Cultural Notes

The concept of propitiation often appears in religious contexts, where sacrifices or offerings are made to appease deities or supernatural forces.

Synonyms for Propitiate

  • Process
  • Proclivity
