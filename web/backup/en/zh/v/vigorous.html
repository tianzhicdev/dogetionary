Vigorous

Pronunciation:ˈvɪɡərəs
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 有力的
  • 精力旺盛的
  • 强壮的

Definitions

adjective

Definition

Characterized by forceful and energetic action or activity.

In ZH

以有力和精力充沛的行动或活动为特征的。

Examples

  • After consuming three cups of coffee, Jack decided to take a vigorous walk, which ended with him being chased by a squirrel!
  • The vigorous debate on social media over pineapple on pizza left even the strongest of friendships in shambles.
  • The new exercise program promises vigorous results, but I just ended up vigorously searching for the remote instead.

Cultural Notes

In English-speaking cultures, being vigorous often has positive connotations, suggesting health, vitality, and activity.

adverb

Definition

In a vigorous manner; energetically.

In ZH

以有力的方式；精力充沛地。

Examples

  • She danced vigorously at the wedding, nearly knocking over the cake in the process!
  • The dog ran vigorously after the mailman, who now believes he is a part-time Olympic sprinter.
  • The politician campaigned vigorously, but not as vigorously as his cat campaigning against the vacuum cleaner.

Cultural Notes

Using 'vigorous' as an adverb can add comedic effect, especially in contexts that contrast the expected energy with a mundane activity.
