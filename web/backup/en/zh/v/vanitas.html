Vanitas

EN→ZH

What Does Vanitas Mean?

  • 虚无
  • 虚妄
  • 无常

Vanitas Pronunciation

/vəˈniːtəs/

Vanitas Definition

noun

Definition

A genre of still-life painting that emphasizes the certainty of death and the vanity of earthly pleasures.

In ZH

一种强调死亡必然性和世俗享乐虚妄的静物画类型。

How to Use Vanitas in a Sentence

  • The vanitas painting depicted a skull beside a wilting flower.
  • In the art gallery, I admired the vanitas works that conveyed deep philosophical meanings.
  • The theme of vanitas is prevalent in many Baroque artworks.
  • A famous vanitas includes symbols of mortality and the fleeting nature of life.
  • Studying vanitas gives insight into historical perspectives on life and death.

Cultural Notes

Vanitas artworks often included symbols like skulls, hourglasses, and rotting fruit to remind viewers of mortality.
