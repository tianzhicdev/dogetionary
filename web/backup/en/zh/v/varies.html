Varies

Pronunciation:ˈvɛriz
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 变化
  • 多样化
  • 差异

Definitions

verb

Definition

To differ in size, amount, degree, or nature from something else of the same general class.

In ZH

在同一类别的其他事物中，在大小、数量、程度或性质上有所不同。

Examples

  • The price of avocados varies wildly depending on the season, one day it's a dollar, the next it's a mortgage!
  • Studies show that people’s pizza toppings vary significantly, but pineapple still remains the controversial king!
  • In the world of fashion, what varies by the season could mean the difference between wearing shorts in the summer and wondering if you should turn on the heat in April!

Cultural Notes

In everyday conversation, 'varies' can be used to express differences in opinions, preferences, or behaviors among people. It shows flexibility and adaptability. For example, you might say, 'My friends’ cooking skills vary; one can barely boil an egg, while another serves Michelin-star quality meals!'.

noun

Definition

A variation or difference in condition, amount, or level, typically within certain limits.

In ZH

在特定限制内条件、数量或水平的变化或差异。

Examples

  • The varies in taste among fast food burgers can be shocking—some are like a mouthful of joy, others taste like regret!
  • Weather in spring varies so much that last week I wore flip-flops, and this week I’m contemplating building an ark!
  • The varies in the color of jellybeans is only surpassed by the varies in opinions about whether they should be served at weddings.

Cultural Notes

Using 'varies' in this context can emphasize the diversity of experiences or qualities. For example, when discussing food, you might say, 'The varies in regional cuisines across China make every meal a delightful surprise!'.
