Valedictory

EN→ZH

What Does Valedictory Mean?

  • 告别的
  • 毕业的
  • 告别辞的

Valedictory Pronunciation

/ˌvælɪˈdɪktəri/

Valedictory Definition

adjective

Definition

Serving as a farewell, especially in speeches or addresses.

In ZH

作为告别的，尤其是在演讲或致辞中。

How to Use Valedictory in a Sentence

  • The valedictory address was both emotional and inspiring.
  • She delivered a valedictory speech at her graduation ceremony.
  • The valedictory message highlighted the achievements of the graduating class.
  • His valedictory remarks were met with applause from the audience.
  • The school principal gave a valedictory message to the graduates.
noun

Definition

A farewell address, especially one delivered at a graduation ceremony.

In ZH

告别演说，尤其是在毕业典礼上发表的演说。

How to Use Valedictory in a Sentence

  • The valedictory of the class was highly praised for its content.
  • During the valedictory, alumni shared their experiences after graduation.
  • The school hosts a special ceremony for the valedictory speech every year.
  • She was selected to give the valedictory due to her outstanding academic performance.
  • His valedictory was memorable and heartfelt.

Synonyms for Valedictory

  • Valetudinarian
  • Valiant
