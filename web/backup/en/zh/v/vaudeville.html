Vaudeville

Pronunciation:/ˈvɔː.də.vɪl/
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 杂耍
  • 小品剧
  • 歌舞杂耍

Definitions

noun

Definition

A theatrical variety show popular in the early 20th century featuring a mixture of specialty acts such as comedians, musicians, dancers, and magicians.

In ZH

一种热门于20世纪初的戏剧杂技表演，包含喜剧演员、音乐家、舞蹈家和魔术师等各种特殊表演的混合。

Examples

  • Vaudeville shows were a popular form of entertainment in America.
  • The vaudeville circuit included many famous performers of the time.
  • She grew up watching her parents perform in vaudeville theaters.
  • The essence of vaudeville lies in its variety and spontaneity.
  • Many early films were inspired by the structure of vaudeville acts.

Cultural Notes

Vaudeville was a precursor to modern forms of entertainment, influencing television variety shows and musicals.
