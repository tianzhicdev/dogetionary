Verbal

Pronunciation:ˈvɜːrbəl
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 口头的
  • 语言的
  • 语言表达的

Definitions

adjective

Definition

Relating to or in the form of words; expressed in spoken or written language.

In ZH

与单词相关的；以口头或书面语言表达的。

Examples

  • She prefers verbal communication over written communication.
  • The instructions were given in a verbal format during the meeting.
  • His verbal skills were impressive during the debate.

Cultural Notes

In many cultures, verbal communication is considered essential for effective interaction, where tone, inflection, and word choice carry significant meaning.

adjective

Definition

Derived from or expressed in words rather than numbers or symbols.

In ZH

来源于或用单词而非数字或符号表达的。

Examples

  • The professor emphasized the value of verbal reasoning in his lectures.
  • In a verbal agreement, the terms are agreed upon through conversation, not written contracts.
  • She was able to relay the information in a verbal manner, making it easy for everyone to understand.

Cultural Notes

In informal settings, verbal agreements can sometimes be respected, but in formal contexts, written agreements are often preferred.

noun

Definition

A form of a word that is derived from a verb, typically used as a noun or adjective.

In ZH

从动词派生出的单词形式，通常用作名词或形容词。

Examples

  • The verbal 'running' can be used as both a noun and a verb in English.
  • In English, verbs can often be transformed into verbals, such as gerunds and participles.
  • Understanding verbals is crucial for mastering English grammar.

Cultural Notes

Many English learners find verbals challenging due to their dual usage as nouns or modifiers, which can lead to confusion with verb forms.
