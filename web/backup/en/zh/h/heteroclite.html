Heteroclite

Pronunciation:/ˈhɛtəroʊˌklaɪt/
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 异类
  • 不规则的事物

Definitions

noun

Definition

A person or thing that deviates from the norm or is irregular in some way.

In ZH

偏离常规或以某种方式不规则的人或事物。

Examples

  • The artist's work was considered heteroclite, standing out from the traditional styles.
  • He was seen as a heteroclite among his peers, always challenging the status quo.
  • The film's heteroclite narrative structure left the audience both confused and intrigued.
  • In a heteroclite society, diversity is not only accepted but celebrated.
  • Her views were heteroclite, often contradicting mainstream opinions.
adjective

Definition

Deviating from the normal or natural type; irregular or unusual.

In ZH

偏离正常或自然类型；不规则或不寻常的。

Examples

  • The heteroclite design of the building made it a landmark in the city.
  • They appreciated the heteroclite nature of the festival, which showcased a variety of cultures.
  • His heteroclite approach to problem-solving often yielded innovative results.
  • Many artists embrace a heteroclite style, blending different influences and ideas.
  • The heteroclite fashion trends of the season surprised many observers.
