Halcyon

EN→ZH

What Does Halcyon Mean?

  • 平静的
  • 宁静的
  • 幸福的

Halcyon Pronunciation

/ˈhæl.si.ən/

Halcyon Definition

noun

Definition

A mythical bird said to calm the wind and waves during winter solstice; also refers to a period of peace and happiness.

In ZH

一种神话中的鸟，传说在冬至期间能平息风浪；也指一段宁静和快乐的时期。

How to Use Halcyon in a Sentence

  • The halcyon days of summer are cherished by all.
  • She reminisced about the halcyon days of her childhood.
  • During the halcyon period, the community thrived without conflict.
  • They sought refuge in the halcyon atmosphere of the countryside.
  • The conversation turned to the halcyon years of the past.

Cultural Notes

In literature, 'halcyon' often symbolizes peace and tranquility, evoking a sense of nostalgia.

adjective

Definition

Calm, peaceful, and happy; a term often used to describe idyllic times in the past.

In ZH

平静、宁静、幸福的；常用来形容过去的美好时光。

How to Use Halcyon in a Sentence

  • They enjoyed a halcyon afternoon by the lake.
  • Halcyon memories filled her mind as she looked through old photographs.
  • The halcyon weather made for a perfect picnic day.
  • In her halcyon dreams, everything was possible.
  • The novel depicts a halcyon era before the chaos of war.

Synonyms for Halcyon

  • Hallowed
  • Hallucinating
  • Hallux
