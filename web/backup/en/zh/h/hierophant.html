Hierophant

Pronunciation:/ˈhaɪərəfənt/
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 教士
  • 神秘学者
  • 祭司

Definitions

noun

Definition

A person, especially a priest in ancient Greece, who interprets sacred mysteries or esoteric principles.

In ZH

一个人，尤其是古希腊的祭司，解读神圣的奥秘或秘传原理。

Examples

  • The hierophant guided the initiates through the ancient rites.
  • In ancient Greece, the hierophant revealed sacred knowledge to the worthy.
  • During the ceremony, the hierophant wore elaborate robes and conducted the rituals.
  • The role of the hierophant was to protect and explain the mysteries of the religion.
  • Many believe the hierophant holds the keys to spiritual enlightenment.

Cultural Notes

In various esoteric traditions, the hierophant symbolizes a teacher or guide in spiritual matters.
