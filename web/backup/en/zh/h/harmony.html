Harmony

Pronunciation:ˈhɑːrməni
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 和谐
  • 协调
  • 一致

Definitions

noun

Definition

A pleasing arrangement of parts, often used in the context of music or design.

In ZH

一种令人愉悦的部分安排，常用于音乐或设计的上下文中。

Examples

  • The harmony in her painting made it very attractive.
  • They sang in perfect harmony, creating a beautiful sound.
  • The architecture of the building reflects a harmony between nature and modern design.

Cultural Notes

In music, harmony refers to the combination of different musical notes played or sung simultaneously to create a pleasing effect. It is a fundamental concept in many cultures' musical traditions.

noun

Definition

The state of being in agreement or concord, often in reference to relationships or social situations.

In ZH

处于一致或和谐状态，常常指人际关系或社会情况。

Examples

  • The committee worked in harmony to achieve their goals.
  • In a successful team, there should be harmony among members.
  • The community thrived due to the harmony between its diverse cultures.

Cultural Notes

In social contexts, harmony is valued in many cultures, especially in Eastern cultures like Chinese, where group cohesion and avoiding conflict are seen as important.

noun

Definition

A balance of different elements, leading to a sense of peace and well-being.

In ZH

不同元素的平衡，从而带来内心的平和与幸福感。

Examples

  • Finding harmony in life can reduce stress.
  • Yoga is often about achieving harmony between body and mind.
  • The garden was designed to create harmony with the surroundings.

Cultural Notes

The concept of harmony is also prevalent in philosophies like Taoism, which emphasizes balance and the interconnectedness of all things.
