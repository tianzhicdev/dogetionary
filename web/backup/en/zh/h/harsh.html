Harsh

Pronunciation:hɑːrʃ
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 严酷的
  • 粗糙的
  • 刺耳的

Definitions

adjective

Definition

Rough or unpleasant to the senses; not smooth or gentle.

In ZH

粗糙或不愉快的感觉；不平滑或温和的。

Examples

  • The harsh wind made it difficult to walk outside.
  • The texture of the fabric was too harsh against my skin.
  • The harsh reality of the situation hit him suddenly.

Cultural Notes

In English, 'harsh' can describe physical sensations, like weather or materials.

adjective

Definition

Severe or unkind in tone or manner; strict or cruel.

In ZH

语气或方式上严厉或不善良；严格或残酷。

Examples

  • Her harsh words made him feel unwelcome.
  • The teacher's harsh criticism discouraged many students.
  • They faced harsh penalties for breaking the law.

Cultural Notes

'Harsh' can also relate to social interactions, indicating a lack of empathy.

adjective

Definition

Difficult or unpleasant to endure; very strong or extreme.

In ZH

难以忍受的或不愉快的；非常强烈或极端的。

Examples

  • The harsh conditions in the desert challenge even the most prepared travelers.
  • His harsh upbringing shaped his views on life.
  • She endured harsh criticism for her controversial opinions.

Cultural Notes

The use of 'harsh' in this context emphasizes the challenges or difficulties faced.
