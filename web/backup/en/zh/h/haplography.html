Haplography

Pronunciation:/ˌhæpləˈɡræfi/
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 单写法
  • 单写现象
  • 单写错误

Definitions

noun

Definition

The phenomenon of writing a word or a part of a word only once instead of multiple times, which can lead to errors or omissions in transcription.

In ZH

只写一个单词或一个词的一部分，而不是多次写，这可能会导致转录中的错误或遗漏。

Examples

  • Haplography often occurs in manuscripts where a scribe mistakenly omits repeated letters.
  • The editor noted the haplography in the text, which distorted the meaning.
  • In ancient texts, haplography can make it difficult to understand the original language.
  • Students learning to transcribe should be aware of haplography as a common mistake.
  • Researchers must consider haplography when analyzing historical documents.

Cultural Notes

Haplography is often discussed in the context of paleography and manuscript studies, where the accuracy of transcriptions is critical.
