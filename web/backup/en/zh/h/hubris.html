Hubris

Pronunciation:/ˈhjuːbrɪs/
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 傲慢
  • 自大
  • 狂妄

Definitions

noun

Definition

Excessive pride or self-confidence, often leading to downfall.

In ZH

过度的自豪或自信，常常导致失败。

Examples

  • His hubris led him to ignore the warnings.
  • The CEO's hubris resulted in the company's bankruptcy.
  • In Greek tragedies, hubris is often a fatal flaw of the hero.
  • She believed her talent was limitless, a classic case of hubris.
  • Hubris can blind individuals to their weaknesses.

Cultural Notes

In ancient Greek literature, hubris often referred to a sense of arrogance before the gods, and was typically seen as a precursor to tragedy.
