Harrow

Pronunciation:/ˈhɛrəʊ/
EN→ZH

Quick Translations

  • 耙
  • 耙子
  • 拖耙

Definitions

noun

Definition

A farming tool used to break up and smooth out the surface of soil.

In ZH

一种用于打破和平整土壤表面的农业工具。

Examples

  • Farmers use a harrow to prepare the fields for planting.
  • The old harrow had seen better days but still functioned well.
  • After plowing, we used a harrow to create a fine seedbed.
  • The harrow helped to aerate the soil effectively.
  • He attached the harrow to the tractor before starting his work.

Cultural Notes

Harrowing is a crucial step in traditional farming that prepares the land for crops.

verb

Definition

To cause distress or torment; to disturb or trouble.

In ZH

使痛苦或折磨；扰乱或困扰。

Examples

  • The news of the disaster harrowed the community.
  • She felt harrowed by the injustice she witnessed.
  • The film’s ending was harrowing and left many in tears.
  • His harrowed expression showed the toll of the long journey.
  • Years of war had harrowed the once vibrant city.

Cultural Notes

In literature, the term 'harrow' is often used to evoke deep emotional responses.
